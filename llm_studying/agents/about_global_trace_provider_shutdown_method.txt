The call to `GLOBAL_TRACE_PROVIDER.shutdown()` cleanly terminates the tracing system by:

1. **Flushing** any spans still buffered in memory to the configured exporters, ensuring no trace data is lost.
2. **Stopping** background workers or threads that the provider or its span processors may have spawned.
3. **Releasing** resources (network connections, file handles, timers) held by exporters and processors.

This is important at application shutdown (or in test teardown) to guarantee that all telemetry is delivered and no stray threads or handles remain.
