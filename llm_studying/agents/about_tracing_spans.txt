A **tracing span** is the fundamental unit of work in a distributed trace. It represents one **operation** or **step** within a larger workflow, and carries the timing and metadata needed to reconstruct how a request flowed through your system. Concretely, a span has:

* **Name**: a human-readable identifier (e.g. `"HTTP GET /users"` or `"db.query:SELECT"`).
* **Timestamps**:

  * **Start time** (when the operation began)
  * **End time** (when it finished)
* **Duration**: computed as `end_time – start_time`.
* **Span ID**: a unique identifier for this span.
* **Parent Span ID** (optional): links this span to its caller, forming a tree or directed acyclic graph of operations.
* **Trace ID**: a globally unique identifier shared by all spans in a single end-to-end trace.
* **Attributes/Tags**: key-value pairs for additional context (e.g. HTTP status code, user ID, database name).
* **Events/Logs** (optional): timestamped annotations or log messages attached to the span.

When you visualize a trace, each span appears as a bar on a timeline, showing precisely how long each operation took and how they overlap. By stitching together spans with shared trace and parent IDs, you get a complete picture of a request’s journey—across threads, processes, and even microservices—so you can pinpoint latency hotspots, errors, and unexpected bottlenecks.
