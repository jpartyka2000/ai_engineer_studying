In databases, a decimal type defined as DECIMAL(10,2) means that the total number of digits (the precision) is 10 and that 2 of those digits are reserved for the fractional part (the scale). In other words, you can have up to 8 digits before the decimal point and 2 digits after it. 

In Pandas, when you see a column with this type (often after reading from a SQL or Delta table), it may be represented as a float (usually float64) or sometimes as an object containing Python Decimal objects if you’ve explicitly converted it. However, the "DECIMAL(10,2)" specification itself comes from the database schema and tells you about the precision and scale of the stored numbers.
