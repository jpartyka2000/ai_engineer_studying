No – once you explicitly stop a Serving Endpoint, its compute is torn down and you stop accruing DBU (compute) charges. The only thing that remains is your model artifacts sitting in the MLflow Model Registry/DBFS (which are just storage‐level costs), but you will not pay for any serving machines or resources while it’s in the “Stopped” state. If you want to eliminate even that minimal storage footprint, you’d need to delete the endpoint (and/or the registered model) entirely.
