Enhanced autoscaling in Databricks automatically adjusts the number of compute resources (or machines) for your job or cluster based on the amount of work that needs to be done. It adds more machines when there's a lot of data to process and removes them when less is needed, saving time and costs.

Here's a simple breakdown:
- **Scaling up**: If your job gets bigger or more complex, enhanced autoscaling quickly adds more machines to handle the increased load.
- **Scaling down**: When the job finishes or slows down, it reduces the number of machines, so you're not paying for unused resources.
- **Efficiency**: It ensures that your job runs faster when needed while avoiding unnecessary costs by automatically adjusting resources up or down.

The "enhanced" part means it does this more smartly than standard autoscaling, by being faster and better at predicting how many machines are actually required. This helps with optimizing performance and cost.
