The **sync mode** (triggered vs. continuous) in Databricks determines how frequently your data and embeddings are synchronized with the vector search index. The choice between the two depends on how often your data changes and how up-to-date your vector index needs to be.

### Triggered Sync Mode
- **How it works**: Synchronization happens only when you explicitly trigger it.
- **Use case**: Best for datasets that are static or rarely updated.
- **Advantages**:
  - You have full control over when updates are synced.
  - Reduces computational overhead and resource usage because syncing is infrequent.
- **When to choose**:
  - If you compute embeddings once and your data in **Column A** is not expected to change often.
  - If you want to manually control when updates to the index occur (e.g., after a batch of new data is added).

### Continuous Sync Mode
- **How it works**: Synchronization happens automatically as new data or updates are made.
- **Use case**: Ideal for datasets that are frequently updated or continuously ingested.
- **Advantages**:
  - Ensures the vector index is always up-to-date with the latest data.
  - No need to manually trigger updates.
- **When to choose**:
  - If your data in **Column A** changes often or is part of a streaming/real-time pipeline.
  - If the index needs to reflect the latest changes immediately for accurate searches.

### Recommendation for Your Use Case
Given your requirement:
- **Triggered Sync** is likely a better fit if embeddings for **Column A** are computed once and won't change often. This avoids unnecessary sync operations and keeps the process efficient.
- Choose **Continuous Sync** only if **Column A** or its embeddings are expected to change regularly, and you need the index to always reflect the most current state.

### Practical Workflow
1. **Triggered Sync**:
   - Compute embeddings for **Column A** once.
   - Trigger a sync after embeddings are generated and ready to be indexed.
2. **Continuous Sync**:
   - Enable this if updates to your dataset or embeddings are frequent and you want the index to remain up-to-date automatically.
