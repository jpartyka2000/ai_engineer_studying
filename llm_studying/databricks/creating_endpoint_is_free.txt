There isn’t a one‐time “endpoint creation” fee in Databricks – you only pay for the compute that backs your serving endpoint (plus any underlying cloud‐provider VM/storage costs). In practice that means:

1. **No license or setup charge** for standing up an endpoint.
2. **You pay Databricks Units (DBUs)** and your cloud-VM minutes **while the endpoint is running**.

   * Each serving endpoint runs on a “serving cluster” whose SKU (and DBU rate) you choose.
   * DBUs are billed by the second; when you scale the endpoint up or down (or it autosuspends if you’ve enabled that), your usage meter follows accordingly.
3. **If you never invoke the endpoint**, it will still incur DBUs as long as the cluster is up, but you can configure idle‐timeout to shut it down and avoid charges when it’s unused.

So you’re free to spin up as many separate endpoints as you like, but each one will incur per-second DBU (and cloud‐VM) charges as soon as it’s active. If you’re trying out a new model or configuration, it’s often cheaper to reuse an existing endpoint or tear down idle ones to minimize billable time.
