You can often get the best of both worlds by **starting with Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT)** to teach your base model the broad behavior you want—and then **refining it with DPO** to align it more tightly to human preferences. Here’s how and why that hybrid approach can boost performance:

---

## 1. Why combine SFT + DPO?

* **SFT lays the foundation**

  * Teaches your model the domain-specific language, desired formats, and general correctness on your data.
  * Turn your prompt–completion pairs into a supervised cross-entropy objective to quickly ingrain “obvious” behavior.

* **DPO sharpens preferences**

  * Takes your human comparisons (A/B preferences) and directly optimizes the model to rank preferred completions higher.
  * Fixes the subtle ordering issues that SFT’s one-size-fits-all loss can’t capture.

Together, you get a model that’s both **competent** (thanks to SFT) and **well-aligned to nuanced human tastes** (thanks to DPO).

---

## 2. Typical workflow

1. **Collect your data**

   * **SFT dataset**: `<prompt, target_completion>` pairs (e.g. high-quality examples).
   * **Preference dataset**: `<prompt, y⁺, y⁻>` triples derived from A/B tests or human ranking.

2. **Stage 1: Supervised Fine-Tuning**

   ```bash
   openai api fine_tunes.create \
     --model base-model \
     --training_file sft_data.jsonl \
     --n_epochs 3 \
     --batch_size 16
   ```

   * Outputs a new checkpoint, e.g. `ft:sft-model-2025-08-15`.

3. **Stage 2: Direct Preference Optimization**

   * Use your **DPO training loop**, but initialize from the SFT checkpoint:

     ```python
     from dpo import DPOTrainer
     model = load_pretrained("ft:sft-model-2025-08-15")
     trainer = DPOTrainer(model, preference_dataset, alpha=1.0)
     trainer.train(epochs=2)
     ```
   * Or, if using OpenAI’s hosted DPO API once available:

     ```json
     POST /v1/fine_tuning/jobs
     {
       "model":"ft:sft-model-2025-08-15",
       "preference_file":"file-xyz",
       "method":"dpo",
       "alpha":1.0
     }
     ```

4. **Deploy & evaluate**

   * You’ll now have `ft:dpo-from-sft-2025-08-15`.
   * Run human evaluations or automatic metrics to verify improvements over pure SFT or pure DPO.

---

## 3. Key benefits

* **Faster convergence**: SFT gets you “in the right ballpark,” so DPO needs fewer epochs to fine-tune preferences.
* **Lower cost**: Less DPO training time (token-based) than doing preference tuning from scratch.
* **Improved stability**: SFT reduces the risk of DPO overfitting to sparse preference data.
* **Clear separation of concerns**: You maintain a clean SFT corpus for broad coverage and a smaller preference set for alignment tweaks.

---

### Takeaway

> **SFT → DPO** is a powerful 2-stage recipe:
>
> 1. **Teach** the model your domain and style with plain supervised learning.
> 2. **Tune** it on human preference data via DPO’s pairwise ranking loss.

The result is a model that’s both **knowledgeable** and **highly aligned** to your users’ nuanced expectations.
