Let’s unpack this term by term.  We’re looking at the probability of seeing a particular trajectory

$$
\tau = (s_0, a_0, s_1, a_1, \dots, s_T)
$$

when we roll out policy $\pi$ in our environment.  The formula is

$$
P(\tau \mid \pi)
= \underbrace{\rho_0(s_0)}_{\substack{\text{prob. of}\\\text{starting at }s_0}}
\;\times\;
\prod_{t=0}^{T-1}
\underbrace{P(s_{t+1}\mid s_t, a_t)}_{\substack{\text{env.\ transition}\\ \text{probability}}}
\;\times\;
\underbrace{\pi(a_t\mid s_t)}_{\substack{\text{policy’s prob.\ of}\\\text{taking }a_t\text{ in }s_t}}
$$

---

### 1. $\rho_0(s_0)$

The probability we begin in state $s_0$.  (Often given by the MDP’s initial‐state distribution.)

### 2. $\prod_{t=0}^{T-1} P(s_{t+1}\mid s_t, a_t)$

At each step $t$, once we take action $a_t$ in state $s_t$, the environment stochastically jumps to $s_{t+1}$ with probability $P(s_{t+1}\mid s_t,a_t)$.  Multiplying these gives the joint chance of that exact sequence of states under the dynamics.

### 3. $\prod_{t=0}^{T-1} \pi(a_t\mid s_t)$

> **This is the “$\pi$” portion you asked about.**

* $\pi(a_t\mid s_t)$ is the **policy**’s probability of choosing action $a_t$ when in state $s_t$.
* If $\pi$ is **stochastic**, it might look like $\pi(\text{“left”}\mid s)=0.7$, $\pi(\text{“right”}\mid s)=0.3$.
* If $\pi$ is **deterministic**, then $\pi(a\mid s)$ is 1 for the chosen action and 0 otherwise.

By multiplying $\pi(a_t\mid s_t)$ at each time $t$, we account for the chance that our policy actually picked those actions along the way.

---

### Putting it all together

1. **Start** in $s_0$ with prob.\ $\rho_0(s_0)$.
2. **At $t=0$**:

   * Policy picks $a_0$ in $s_0$ with prob.\ $\pi(a_0\mid s_0)$.
   * Environment transitions to $s_1$ with prob.\ $P(s_1\mid s_0,a_0)$.
3. **At $t=1$**:

   * Policy picks $a_1$ in $s_1$ with prob.\ $\pi(a_1\mid s_1)$.
   * Env.\ goes to $s_2$ with prob.\ $P(s_2\mid s_1,a_1)$.
4. **…and so on** until $t=T-1$.

The product of all those terms is precisely the probability of observing that exact $(s_0,a_0,s_1,a_1,\dots,s_T)$ under policy $\pi$.
