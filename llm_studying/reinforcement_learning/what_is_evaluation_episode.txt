An “evaluation episode” is simply one **complete rollout** of your agent in the environment, from start to finish—i.e.:

1. **Reset** the env (e.g. `obs, _ = env.reset()`).
2. **Loop**:

   * Use your policy to pick an action (e.g. `action, _ = model.predict(obs, deterministic=True)`).
   * Step the env (`obs, reward, terminated, truncated, info = env.step(action)`),
   * Accumulate that reward.
3. **Stop** as soon as the env signals either `terminated=True` (natural end) or `truncated=True` (hit a max-length/time limit).

That whole sequence of **reset → step → … → done** counts as **one** evaluation episode.  When you call

```python
evaluate_policy(model, env, n_eval_episodes=30)
```

it simply runs 30 of those full rollouts (without learning), records the total reward per rollout, and then returns their mean and standard deviation.
