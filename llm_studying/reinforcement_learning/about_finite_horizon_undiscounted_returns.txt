### Finite‑Horizon Undiscounted Returns

When you care only about rewards over a fixed episode length $T$ and treat all rewards equally (no discounting), you use **finite‑horizon undiscounted returns**.

---

#### 1. Definition

For a trajectory $(s_0,a_0,r_1,\dots,s_T)$, the **undiscounted return** from time $t$ is

$$
G_t^{(T)} \;=\; \sum_{k=0}^{T-t-1} r_{t+k+1}
\;=\; r_{t+1} + r_{t+2} + \cdots + r_T.
$$

No factor $\gamma$ appears—each reward contributes equally, and you stop summing at the fixed horizon $T$.

---

#### 2. Value Functions

* **State‑value** under policy $\pi$:

  $$
    V_t^\pi(s)
    = \mathbb{E}\bigl[G_t^{(T)} \mid s_t = s,\;\pi\bigr].
  $$

  Note that $V_t^\pi$ generally **depends on** the current time step $t$ because fewer rewards remain as $t$ approaches $T$.

* **Action‑value** under $\pi$:

  $$
    Q_t^\pi(s,a)
    = \mathbb{E}\bigl[G_t^{(T)} \mid s_t=s,\,a_t=a,\;\pi\bigr].
  $$

---

#### 3. Bellman Equations (Undiscounted)

For $t < T$, the recursive form becomes

$$
V_t^\pi(s)
= \mathbb{E}_{a\sim\pi(\cdot\mid s)}\Bigl[
    r_{t+1} + V_{t+1}^\pi(s_{t+1})
  \Bigr],
$$

with boundary condition
$\displaystyle V_T^\pi(s)\;=\;0$ for all terminal states.

---

#### 4. When to Use

* **Strictly episodic tasks** where you care about total reward in a fixed-length game or trial.
* **Monte Carlo estimation** of returns: you simply sum rewards until the episode’s end.
* **Finite‑horizon planning** (dynamic programming) via backward induction without discount factors.

---

#### 5. Practical Considerations

* **Time‑dependent policy**: the optimal action may vary with how many steps remain.
* **No convergence issues**: unlike $\gamma<1$, you don’t worry about infinite sums.
* **Variance**: undiscounted returns may have higher variance, since distant rewards count as much as immediate ones.

---

### Bottom Line

> Finite‑horizon undiscounted returns sum up all future rewards equally until a fixed end of the episode. They are the natural choice in tasks where every reward step matters the same and episodes terminate in a known number of steps.
