Almost—but even stronger: for any real $x$,

$$
e^x > 0.
$$

* **Strictly positive**: $e^x$ never hits 0, it just gets arbitrarily close as $x\to -\infty$.
* **Range**: $(0,\infty)$, not $[0,\infty)$.

**Quick checks:**

* $e^1 \approx 2.72$
* $e^0 = 1$
* $e^{-1} \approx 0.37$

No matter how negative $x$ is, $e^x$ remains positive (it “decays” toward 0 but never reaches it).
