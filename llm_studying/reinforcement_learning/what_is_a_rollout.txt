In RL speak, a **rollout** (sometimes called a **trajectory** or **episode**) is simply the process of “letting your agent act in the environment under its current policy, from some start state until termination (or a fixed time horizon), and recording what happens.”

Concretely, a rollout gives you a sequence like:

```
state₀, action₀, reward₁, state₁, action₁, reward₂, …, state_T
```

– you begin in an initial state state₀
– at each t you sample actionₜ ∼ π(·|stateₜ) (your policy)
– you step the env: (stateₜ, actionₜ) → (rewardₜ₊₁, stateₜ₊₁)
– you stop once you hit a terminal state or max steps

Rollouts are how you:

1. **Estimate returns** (sum of future rewards) for policy-gradient methods.
2. **Gather new on-policy data** (SARSA, PPO, REINFORCE).
3. **Populate your replay buffer** in off-policy methods (DQN, SAC).

Every time you “roll out” you get fresh samples of how your policy actually behaves, which you then use to update your value- or policy-networks.
