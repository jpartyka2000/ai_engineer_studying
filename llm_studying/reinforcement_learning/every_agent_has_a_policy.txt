Yes—at its core, **every** RL agent is defined by a **policy**, which is simply the rule (or function) it uses to pick actions given what it observes.

* **Explicit policies**: in policy-based or actor–critic methods you literally parameterize $\pi_\theta(a\mid s)$, so your “actor” network is your policy.
* **Implicit policies**: even in pure value-based methods (like Q-learning or DQN), you still need a way to choose actions from your $Q$-values—typically

  * **greedy**: $a=\arg\max_a Q(s,a)$ (a deterministic policy), or
  * **ε-greedy**: with probability ε pick random, otherwise pick $\arg\max$ (a stochastic policy).

So whether it’s hand-coded, learned directly, or derived from a value function, every agent has a policy that tells it “in state s, do a.”
