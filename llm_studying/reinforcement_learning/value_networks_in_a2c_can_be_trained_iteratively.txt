2️⃣ Monte Carlo Returns Are Sometimes Used Initially

Some implementations combine Monte Carlo returns and TD targets (or use multi-step returns, e.g., TD(λ)):

y_t = r_t + \gamma r_{t+1} + \dots + \gamma^{n-1} r_{t+n-1} + \gamma^n V_\theta(s_{t+n})

Early on, you may rely more on actual sampled returns to guide learning.

As the value network gets better, the bootstrapped part  becomes more reliable.


This approach reduces bias in early training when the critic is not yet accurate.


---

3️⃣ Practical Strategy

No separate pre-training: Most A2C implementations start with a randomly initialized value network and train it online from the first steps.

Advantage estimates help: The actor’s policy updates depend on the advantage function . Even rough value predictions provide useful learning signals to reduce variance compared to vanilla policy gradients.

Bootstrapping quality improves naturally: As both actor and critic improve, targets become more accurate and stable.



---

✅ Key takeaway:
You don't need to train the critic offline first. TD learning is self-correcting: early targets are noisy but improve over time as the critic learns from experience. Pre-training on episode data can help warm-start learning, but it's not required for A2C to work.
