No—it doesn’t.

* An **epoch** means you’ve run **through the entire dataset** (or full batch of collected rollouts) once.
* A **mini-batch** is just one small slice of that data.

So when you:

1. Split your data of size $N$ into $M$ mini-batches (each of size $\approx N/M$),
2. And then you do $M$ forward-and-backward passes—one on each mini-batch—

that whole sequence of $M$ updates constitutes **one epoch**.  Each individual mini-batch pass is **one iteration** inside that epoch, not an epoch by itself.
