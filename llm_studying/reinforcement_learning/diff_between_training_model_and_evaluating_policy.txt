Training the model with

```python
model.learn(total_timesteps=5000)
```

means you’re running the reinforcement‐learning algorithm (here PPO) to **update** the policy’s parameters.  During training the agent:

1. **Interacts** with the environment according to its current policy (plus any exploration noise).
2. **Collects trajectories** (state → action → reward → next state).
3. **Computes gradients** (using PPO’s clipped objective) and **tunes** the policy network weights to improve expected return.

All of that happens online, over 5 000 time-steps in your example.

---

Evaluating the policy with

```python
mean_reward, std_reward = evaluate_policy(model, env, n_eval_episodes=30)
```

means you’re **not** learning any more—you’re simply running your **final** policy **without** gradient updates (and usually with exploration turned off) for 30 full episodes, and measuring:

* **`mean_reward`**: the average total reward per episode
* **`std_reward`**: how much that episodic return varies

That gives you a clean estimate of how well your trained policy actually performs, rather than how well it’s still exploring or fitting to the training signal.
