Exactly—that’s right.

* **Logits** $z_j$ are just unconstrained real numbers output by your network. There’s no requirement that $\sum_j z_j = 1$ (or even that they’re positive).
* **Softmax** turns those logits into a valid probability distribution by exponentiating and normalizing:

  $$
    \pi_\theta(a=k\mid s)
    = \frac{\exp(z_k)}{\sum_{j=1}^K \exp(z_j)},
  $$

  which guarantees $\pi_\theta(a\mid s)\ge0$ for all $a$ and $\sum_{k=1}^K\pi_\theta(a=k\mid s)=1$.

Without softmax, you wouldn’t have a proper categorical distribution to sample from or compute log-likelihoods on.
