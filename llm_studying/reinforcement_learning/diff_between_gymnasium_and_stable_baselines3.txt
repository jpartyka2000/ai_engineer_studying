At a high level, **Gymnasium** and **Stable-Baselines3** serve two complementary roles in the RL ecosystem:

---

### Gymnasium

* **What it is**: A library of RL environments and a standardized API for them (the successor to OpenAI Gym).
* **Provides**:

  * **Environments** (classic control, Atari, MuJoCo, etc.) you can step through with `env.reset()`, `env.step(action)`, and inspect `obs, reward, done, info`.
  * **Wrappers** and utilities (e.g. vectorized environments, observation/action transforms, monitoring).
* **Dependency**: Pure Python, with optional C-backends for fast simulations (e.g. MuJoCo).

Use Gymnasium when you want to:

1. **Define or load** an environment for your agent to interact with.
2. **Build custom** environments (by subclassing `gymnasium.Env`).
3. **Run simple baselines** or debug your agent’s interactions.

---

### Stable-Baselines3 (SB3)

* **What it is**: A library of **ready-made, well-tested RL algorithms** implemented on top of PyTorch, designed to plug directly into Gym/Gymnasium environments.
* **Provides**:

  * **Algorithms**: PPO, DQN, A2C, SAC, TD3, etc., with sensible default hyperparameters.
  * **Training loops**, logging, checkpointing, and evaluation utilities.
  * **Callbacks** for curriculum learning, early stopping, custom logging.
* **Dependency**: PyTorch and a Gym(nasium)-compatible environment.

Use SB3 when you want to:

1. **Train** an agent without re-implementing the algorithm yourself.
2. **Benchmark** different approaches on the same environment.
3. **Leverage** built-in tools for logging (TensorBoard, Weights & Biases), vectorized rollouts, and hyperparameter tuning.

---

### In Practice

```python
import gymnasium as gym
from stable_baselines3 import PPO

# 1) Create an environment
env = gym.make("CartPole-v1")

# 2) Instantiate a ready-made agent
model = PPO("MlpPolicy", env, verbose=1)

# 3) Train it
model.learn(total_timesteps=100_000)

# 4) Evaluate / predict
obs, _ = env.reset()
action, _ = model.predict(obs)
```

* **Gymnasium** gives you the `env`.
* **Stable-Baselines3** gives you the `model` that learns from that `env`.

---

### Summary

* **Gymnasium** = *“worlds to play in”*.
* **SB3** = *“hands-off implementations of brain-power”* to master those worlds.
