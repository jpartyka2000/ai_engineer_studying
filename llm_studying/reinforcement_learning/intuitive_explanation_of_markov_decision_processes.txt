A **Markov Decision Process** (MDP) is a mathematical framework for modeling any situation where an **agent** makes a sequence of **decisions** under uncertainty, aiming to maximize some notion of cumulative **reward**.  Intuitively, an MDP is defined by:

1. **States ($S$)**
   – All the “situations” the agent can find itself in.
   – E.g., in a game: the board configuration; in navigation: your current location and speed.

2. **Actions ($A$)**
   – All the choices the agent can make in each state.
   – E.g., move left/right, pass the ball, accelerate or brake.

3. **Transition dynamics ($P$)**
   – A rule (often stochastic) that says, if you’re in state $s$ and take action $a$, you’ll next land in state $s'$ with probability $P(s'\!\mid s,a)$.
   – Captures the world’s randomness (missed passes, slippery roads, opponent moves).

4. **Rewards ($r$)**
   – A signal the environment gives after each action, $r(s,a,s')$, measuring how “good” that particular transition was.
   – E.g., +1 for scoring, –1 for collision, 0 otherwise.

5. **Markov property**
   – The key assumption: **“the future depends only on the present, not the past.”**
   – Formally, given the current state $s$, the next state $s'$ and reward $r$ depend only on $s$ and the action $a$, not on how you arrived at $s$.

6. **Objective: discounted return**
   – The agent seeks a policy $\pi$ (a mapping from states to actions) that maximizes the expected sum of future rewards, usually with a **discount factor** ($\gamma<1$) that makes near‑term rewards count more than far‑future ones.

---

### Why it matters

* **Unified view:** Games, robotics, finance, and more can all be expressed as MDPs.
* **Reinforcement learning:** All RL algorithms (value iteration, Q‑learning, policy gradients) are ways to solve—or approximate solutions for—an underlying MDP.
* **Decision‑making under uncertainty:** Captures both the **agent’s control** (via actions) and the **world’s randomness** (via transitions), while providing a clear goal (maximize reward).

---

**In one sentence:**
An MDP is a “board game” played against nature—where at each turn you pick an action based solely on your current position, nature responds randomly, you get a reward, and you repeat, always respecting the principle that “what happens next depends only on where you are and what you do now.”
