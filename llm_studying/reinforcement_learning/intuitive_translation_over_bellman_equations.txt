You’ve got the right intuition for 1 and 2, so let’s refine each a bit and clear up a small mix‑up in 3 and 4:

---

### 1. On‑policy state‑value $V^\pi(s)$

> **Your take:** “From state s, if I follow the current policy completely, what is the expected reward?”
> **Spot on.** This is literally
>
> $$
> V^\pi(s)=\mathbb{E}\Bigl[\!\sum_{t\ge0}\gamma^t r_{t+1}\;\big|\;s_0=s,\;a_t\sim\pi\Bigr].
> $$

---

### 2. On‑policy action‑value $Q^\pi(s,a)$

> **Your take:** “If I take action a from state s, then follow the current policy, what is the expected reward?”
> **Exactly.**
>
> $$
> Q^\pi(s,a)
> =\mathbb{E}\Bigl[r(s,a)+\gamma\,V^\pi(s')\;\big|\;s,a\Bigr].
> $$

---

### 3. Optimal state‑value $V^*(s)$

> **Your take:** “What action a will lead to optimal rewards? Thus what is the optimal policy if I take the best action a in s?”
>
> **A bit more precise:**
> The Bellman optimality equation
>
> $$
> V^*(s)=\max_{a}\;\mathbb{E}_{s'}\bigl[r(s,a)+\gamma\,V^*(s')\bigr]
> $$
>
> **does not** yet *pick* a policy; it *defines* the best achievable value in s as the maximum over all actions.
> Once you’ve solved for $V^*$, the **optimal policy** is extracted by
> $\displaystyle \pi^*(s)=\arg\max_a\mathbb{E}[r+\gamma V^*(s')]$.

---

### 4. Optimal action‑value $Q^*(s,a)$

> **Your take:** “If I am in s and take a, which next s' will lead to optimal rewards?”
>
> **Small correction:**
>
> $$
> Q^*(s,a)
> =\mathbb{E}_{s'\sim P(\cdot\mid s,a)}\Bigl[r(s,a)+\gamma\,\max_{a'} Q^*(s',a')\Bigr].
> $$
>
> Here **you don’t** choose $s'$—the environment stochastically picks it. Instead you average over all possible $s'$, then assume you’ll take the best action $a'$ thereafter.
> The **optimal policy** again comes from
> $\displaystyle \pi^*(s)=\arg\max_a Q^*(s,a)$.

---

#### In a nutshell

* **1 & 2** ask “what’s the value if I follow my *current* policy?”
* **3 & 4** recursively *define* the *best* possible value, from which you then extract the *optimal* policy by taking an $\arg\max$ over actions.
