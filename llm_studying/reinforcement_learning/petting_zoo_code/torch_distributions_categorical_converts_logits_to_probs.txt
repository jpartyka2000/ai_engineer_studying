They’re never explicitly turned into a separate `probs` tensor in your code—PyTorch’s `Categorical` distribution handles that for you under the hood.

When you do:

```python
dist = Categorical(logits=logits)
```

1. `Categorical` **stores** your raw `logits` tensor.
2. Whenever you call

   * `dist.sample()`, it (conceptually) converts them to probabilities via

     $$
       p_i = \frac{\exp(\text{logit}_i)}{\sum_j \exp(\text{logit}_j)}
     $$

     then draws an index according to that categorical pmf.
   * `dist.log_prob(a)`, it again uses those logits to compute
     $\log p_a = \text{logit}_a - \log\sum_j\exp(\text{logit}_j)$
     (i.e.\ `log_softmax(logits)[a]`) and returns that.

So the softmax/normalization step is all buried inside `torch.distributions.Categorical`—you just feed it logits and it takes care of turning them into a proper probability distribution whenever it needs to sample or compute log-probs.
