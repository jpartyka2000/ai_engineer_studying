A “parameter” in PyTorch isn’t the whole layer—it’s each individual **trainable tensor** inside that layer (e.g. the weight matrix or the bias vector), not a single node or neuron.

* When you do

  ```python
  layer = nn.Linear( in_features=10, out_features=5 )
  ```

  under the hood you get two `nn.Parameter` objects:

  * `layer.weight` of shape `(5, 10)`
  * `layer.bias`   of shape `(5,)`

* Calling `model.parameters()` yields **all** those Weight and Bias tensors (and any other `nn.Parameter` you’ve added), one by one.

So:

* **Layers** (`nn.Linear`, `nn.Conv2d`, etc.) are **modules** that **contain** parameters.
* **Parameters** are the actual tensors (`weight`, `bias`, …) you optimize.

There are no “per-node” parameters—each connection (edge) in your network has a weight, and typically each neuron has one bias; those weights and biases are the `Parameter` objects you see.
