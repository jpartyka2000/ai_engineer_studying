MuJoCo (short for **Multi-Joint dynamics with Contact**) is a high-performance, general-purpose physics engine widely used in robotics, biomechanics, and machine-learning research:

* **Core focus**
  It provides fast, accurate simulation of articulated bodies with complex contact dynamics, using complementarity-based solvers for contacts and constraints. Models are typically specified in XML, and you can simulate everything from simple manipulators to legged robots in rich environments. ([Wikipedia][1])

* **History & licensing**
  Originally described in 2012 by Todorov et al., it was commercialized by Roboti LLC and acquired by Google DeepMind in October 2021. In May 2022 DeepMind open-sourced MuJoCo under the Apache 2.0 license. ([Wikipedia][1])

* **Implementations & bindings**
  The engine is implemented in C/C++ for performance, with bindings available in Python (and C#), making it easy to integrate with reinforcement-learning libraries (e.g. Gymnasium’s MuJoCo environments) and deep-learning frameworks. ([Wikipedia][1])

* **Ecosystem & use**
  Parts of the DeepMind Control Suite use MuJoCo under the hood. In the wider community, it’s a de facto standard simulator for benchmarking RL algorithms, thanks to its combination of speed, fidelity, and ease of use.

* **Current release**
  As of January 15, 2025, the latest stable version is **3.2.7**. ([Wikipedia][1])
