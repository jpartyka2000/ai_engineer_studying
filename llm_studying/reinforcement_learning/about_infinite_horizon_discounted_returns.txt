### Infinite‑Horizon Discounted Returns

When tasks don’t have a natural endpoint—or you want your agent to optimize over an unbounded future—you use **infinite‑horizon discounted returns**.

---

#### 1. Definition

For $\gamma\in[0,1)$ and an (infinite) trajectory $(s_0,a_0,r_1,s_1,a_1,r_2,\dots)$, the **discounted return** from time $t$ is

$$
G_t \;=\; \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} \gamma^k\,r_{t+k+1}
\;=\; r_{t+1} + \gamma\,r_{t+2} + \gamma^2\,r_{t+3} + \cdots.
$$

* **$\gamma$** down‑weights distant rewards so that the infinite sum **converges**.
* When $\gamma$ is close to 1, you care almost equally about far‑off rewards; when small, you focus on immediate gains.

---

#### 2. Value Functions

* **State–value** under policy $\pi$:

  $$
    V^\pi(s)
    = \mathbb{E}\bigl[G_t \mid s_t = s,\,\pi\bigr].
  $$

  Crucially, **no dependence on $t$**—once you’re in a state, the same future‑looking return applies regardless of “what step” you’re on.

* **Action–value** under $\pi$:

  $$
    Q^\pi(s,a)
    = \mathbb{E}\bigl[G_t \mid s_t=s,\,a_t=a,\,\pi\bigr].
  $$

---

#### 3. Bellman Equations

For all states $s$,

$$
V^\pi(s)
= \mathbb{E}_{a\sim\pi(\cdot\mid s)}\bigl[r_{1}
  + \gamma\,V^\pi(s')\bigr],
$$

where $s'\sim P(\cdot\mid s,a)$.  In operator form:

$$
V^\pi = \mathcal{T}^\pi V^\pi,
\quad
\mathcal{T}^\pi V(s)
\;=\;\mathbb{E}_{a,r,s'}\bigl[r + \gamma\,V(s')\bigr].
$$

Because $\gamma<1$, $\mathcal{T}^\pi$ is a **contraction**, guaranteeing a **unique** fixed point $V^\pi$.

---

#### 4. Why Discount and Infinite?

* **Convergence:** ensures $\sum\gamma^k r$ is finite.
* **Stationarity:** policies and value functions become **time‑independent**, simplifying learning and planning.
* **Trade‑off:**

  * High $\gamma\approx1$: long effective horizon, but higher variance in estimates.
  * Low $\gamma$: more myopic, lower variance but potential bias against long‑term payoff.

---

#### 5. Algorithms

* **Dynamic Programming:** policy evaluation via repeated Bellman backups until convergence.
* **Temporal‑Difference Learning:** update $V(s)$ toward $r + \gamma V(s')$.
* **Q‑Learning / SARSA:** learn $Q(s,a)$ similarly via $r + \gamma \max_{a'}Q(s',a')$ or on‑policy.
* **Policy Gradient:** optimize the expected discounted return
  $\;J(\theta)=\mathbb{E}\bigl[\sum\gamma^k r_{k+1}\bigr]$
  using sampled trajectories.

---

### Bottom Line

> Infinite‑horizon discounted returns let you optimize behavior over an unbounded future in a **stationary**, **convergent** framework. The discount factor $\gamma$ balances long‑term objectives against learning stability and variance.
