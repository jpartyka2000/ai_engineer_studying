Yes—there’s nothing magic tying a rollout to a fixed length. A “rollout” simply means “follow the policy in the env and record everything until you stop,” and that stopping criterion can be:

* the natural end of an episode (a terminal state),
* a fixed time‐horizon you choose (e.g. 200 steps),
* or any other cutoff you impose.

So each rollout produces a sequence

```
state₀, action₀, reward₁, state₁, action₁, reward₂, …, state_T
```

of **T** transitions, and **T** can be whatever you need.  If your environment doesn’t terminate early, you might collect N steps per rollout; if it ends sooner, you get fewer.  In short, rollouts can contain an arbitrary number of actions + rewards, subject only to your chosen stopping rule.
