In Gymnasium (the successor to classic Gym), the base `Env.reset()` does a couple of important things for you:

1. **Seeding**
   When you call

   ```python
   obs, info = env.reset(seed=42)
   ```

   under the hood `super().reset(seed=42)` will take care of initializing the environment’s random number generator(s) with that seed.  If you skip it, your own use of `random.randrange(...)` (or any other RNG inside your env) won’t actually be reproducible.

2. **Wrapper compatibility**
   If someone wraps your env in one of Gym’s built-in wrappers (e.g. `TimeLimit`, `RecordVideo`, `Monitor`), those wrappers often override `reset()` and do stuff *before* or *after* your `reset()`.  By calling `super().reset(...)` you ensure that the wrapper chain all gets the reset call it needs.

3. **API conformance**
   The new Gymnasium API requires `reset()` to return `(obs, info)` rather than just `obs`.  The base class handles the empty‐`info` case, so you don’t have to re-implement that boilerplate.

So in short, calling:

```python
def reset(self, *, seed=None, options=None):
    super().reset(seed=seed)        # ← seed the RNG and let wrappers hook in
    self.current = random.randrange(self.n_questions)
    …
    return obs, {}
```

is the recommended pattern to:

1. Wire up seeding properly,
2. Let any outer wrappers do their work,
3. Adhere to the Gymnasium `(obs, info)` signature.

If you omit that call, your environment may not behave deterministically under `seed=…`, and wrapped environments might mis-function.
