A **value-based method** in RL is one that learns a **value function**—most commonly an action-value function $Q(s,a)$—instead of learning the policy $\pi(a\mid s)$ directly. The value function estimates “how good” it is to take action $a$ in state $s$ (usually in terms of expected future reward).

* **Examples**: Q-learning, SARSA, Deep Q-Networks (DQN)
* **How a policy emerges**: once you have $Q(s,a)$, you derive (implicitly) a policy by, say,

  * **greedy**: $\pi(s)=\arg\max_a Q(s,a)$
  * **$\varepsilon$-greedy**: with prob $\varepsilon$ pick a random action, otherwise pick $\arg\max_a Q(s,a)$

So a value-based method doesn’t *parameterize* $\pi$ directly, but it *does* define one implicitly via a rule applied to the learned values.
