In that context, **$K$** is simply the number of discrete actions (or “categories”) your policy can choose from.

* Your policy network outputs a logit vector

  $$
    z = f_\theta(s)\in\mathbb{R}^K
  $$

  of length $K$, where each component $z_j$ corresponds to one of the $K$ possible actions.
* The softmax then turns those $K$ logits into a valid probability distribution over those $K$ actions:

  $$
    \pi_\theta(a=k\mid s)
    = \frac{e^{z_k}}{\sum_{j=1}^K e^{z_j}}.
  $$
