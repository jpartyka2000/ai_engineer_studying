In model‑free policy optimization, you’re directly searching for a policy $\pi_\theta$ (parameterized by $\theta$) without ever learning a model of $P$ or $r$.  The main goals driving those algorithms are:

1. **Maximize expected return**

   $$
     J(\theta) = \mathbb{E}_{\tau\sim\pi_\theta}\Bigl[\sum_{t=0}^\infty \gamma^t\,r_{t+1}\Bigr]
   $$

   The core objective is to adjust $\theta$ so that, on average, the agent collects as much (discounted) reward as possible.

2. **Balance exploration vs.\ exploitation**

   * Ensure the policy remains stochastic (or sufficiently noisy) during training so you keep gathering informative data about untried actions or states.
   * Yet gradually focus probability mass on high‑value actions to improve performance.

3. **Reduce gradient variance**

   * Raw REINFORCE updates
     $\nabla_\theta J = \mathbb{E}[\nabla_\theta\log\pi_\theta(a|s)\,G_t]$
     can have very high variance.
   * Methods (e.g. actor‑critic, advantage functions, GAE) introduce baselines and critics to stabilize and speed convergence.

4. **Ensure stable, monotonic improvement**

   * Algorithms like **TRPO** or **PPO** constrain how much $\pi_\theta$ can change each update (via KL‑penalties or clipped objectives), preventing catastrophic policy swings.
   * The goal is steady, reliable progress rather than oscillations or collapse.

5. **Be sample‑efficient**

   * Since every environment interaction can be costly, policy optimizers seek to get the most learning signal out of each trajectory via techniques like importance sampling, off‑policy correction, replay buffers, or trust‑region methods.

6. **Scale to high‑dimensional spaces**

   * Use powerful function approximators (e.g. deep nets) but design losses and optimizers that cope with large parameter counts, continuous action spaces, and partial observability.

---

**In summary:** model‑free policy optimization aims to **directly tune** a policy to *reliably* and *efficiently* maximize expected returns, while managing exploration, controlling variance, and ensuring stable, sample‑efficient updates in complex environments.
