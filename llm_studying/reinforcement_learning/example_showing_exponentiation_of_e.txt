Here’s how you can see, term by term, that

$$
e^{0.1}\;\ne\;\frac1{e^{0.1}}
$$

and in fact really is about 1.10517:

---

## 1. The exponential series

By definition,

$$
e^x \;=\;\sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{x^n}{n!}.
$$

Plug in $x=0.1$:

| $n$ | term $\tfrac{(0.1)^n}{n!}$             | numeric      | cumulative sum |
| :-: | :------------------------------------- | :----------- | :------------- |
|  0  | $\tfrac{(0.1)^0}{0!}=1$                | 1.0000000000 | 1.0000000000   |
|  1  | $\tfrac{(0.1)^1}{1!}=0.1$              | 0.1000000000 | 1.1000000000   |
|  2  | $\tfrac{(0.1)^2}{2!}=0.005$            | 0.0050000000 | 1.1050000000   |
|  3  | $\tfrac{(0.1)^3}{3!}\approx0.00016667$ | 0.000166667  | 1.105166667    |
|  4  | $\tfrac{(0.1)^4}{4!}\approx0.00000417$ | 0.000004167  | 1.105170833    |
|  5  | $\tfrac{(0.1)^5}{5!}\approx0.00000008$ | 0.000000083  | 1.105170917    |

Even just five terms already give

$$
e^{0.1}\approx1.105170917.
$$

---

## 2. The reciprocal

By contrast,

$$
\frac1{e^{0.1}}
= e^{-0.1}
$$

has the series

$$
e^{-0.1}
=\sum_{n=0}^\infty\frac{(-0.1)^n}{n!}
\approx 1 - 0.1 + 0.005 - 0.0001667 + \dots
\approx 0.904837.
$$

---

### Bottom line

* **$e^{0.1}\approx1.10517$** (sum of positive terms).
* **$1/e^{0.1}=e^{-0.1}\approx0.90484$** (alternating series).

They’re reciprocals of one another—but clearly not equal!
