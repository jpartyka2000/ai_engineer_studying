Not quite—here’s the breakdown:

```tex
V^{\pi}(s) = \mathbb{E}_{\substack{a\sim\pi(\cdot\!\mid\!s)\\s'\sim P(\cdot\!\mid\!s,a)}}\bigl[r(s,a)\;+\;\gamma\,V^{\pi}(s')\bigr]
```

* **$a\sim\pi(\cdot\mid s)$** means **the policy** $\pi$ gives you a probability distribution over actions in state $s$, and you draw $a$ from that.
* **$s'\sim P(\cdot\mid s,a)$** means **the environment** (not the policy) has a transition kernel $P$.  That kernel assigns, for each $(s,a)$, a distribution over next states $s'$.  You draw $s'$ from **that** distribution.

So:

* **Policy $\pi$** → samples **actions**.
* **Dynamics $P$** → samples **next states**.

The Bellman equation then averages over both sources of randomness: “first pick $a$ according to $\pi$, then pick $s'$ according to $P$, and compute the immediate reward plus discounted future value.”
