Here’s how those three terms are used in RL literature—there’s a lot of overlap, but each carries a slightly different emphasis:

---

## 1. Trajectory

* **Definition:** A (finite or infinite) sequence of the form

  $$
    \tau = (s_0, a_0, r_1, s_1, a_1, r_2, \dots)
  $$

  where each $s_t$ is a state, $a_t$ an action, and $r_{t+1}$ the reward received after $(s_t,a_t)$.
* **Notes:**

  * Often used interchangeably with **“path”** or **“rollout”**.
  * Can be truncated (e.g. first $T$ steps) even in continuing tasks with no natural end.

---

## 2. Episode

* **Definition:** A trajectory that **begins** in some start state $s_0$ and **terminates** at a designated terminal state $s_T$.
* **Key feature:**

  * There is a well‑defined **end** (e.g. game over, reaching a goal, hitting a max time step).
  * The episode’s length $T$ can vary.
* **Use cases:**

  * Standard in **episodic** tasks like games or navigation, where you collect return $G = \sum_{t=0}^{T-1} \gamma^t r_{t+1}.$

---

## 3. Rollout

* **Definition:** The process of running (or “rolling out”) a policy $\pi$ in the environment to **generate** a trajectory or episode.
* **Emphasis:**

  * **Action:** “Perform a rollout” means “simulate forward under $\pi$.”
  * **Result:** you get one (or many) trajectories/episodes.
* **Variants:**

  * **On‑policy rollout:** using the current policy $\pi$.
  * **Off‑policy rollout:** using a different behavior policy $\mu$, but still collecting $(s,a,r)$ tuples.

---

### How they relate

| Term           | Focus                                                          | Typical context                                                             |
| -------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Trajectory** | The raw sequence of $(s,a,r)$ (possibly infinite)              | Theoretical definitions; function approximators operating on partial traces |
| **Episode**    | A trajectory with a clear start **and** end                    | Episodic tasks, Monte Carlo estimation, episode‑based evaluations           |
| **Rollout**    | The **act** of generating trajectories/episodes under a policy | Data collection, simulation loops in policy evaluation or training          |

---

#### Example workflow

1. **Rollout** your policy $\pi$ for $N$ episodes.
2. Each **rollout** yields an **episode** $\tau^{(i)}$ that terminates at $T_i$.
3. You then treat each episode as a **trajectory** sample for updating your value estimates or policy.

---

**Bottom line:**

* A **trajectory** is the abstract sequence of experience.
* An **episode** is a trajectory that ends when the task terminates.
* A **rollout** is the act of producing those trajectories/episodes by simulating your policy in the environment.
