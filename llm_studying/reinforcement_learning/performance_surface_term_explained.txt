Exactly—that “surface” is just the function

$$
J(\theta) \;=\;\mathbb{E}_{\tau\sim\pi_\theta}\bigl[R(\tau)\bigr],
$$

viewed as a “height” over the space of all possible parameter vectors $\theta$.

* For **any** $\theta$, $J(\theta)$ tells you the **expected return** your policy $\pi_\theta$ would get.
* As you change $\theta$, you move around on that landscape: some directions lead you uphill (better performance), some downhill (worse).
* At iteration $k$, you’re standing at $\theta_k$ on that same unchanging landscape—you compute (or estimate) the **local slope** $\nabla J(\theta_k)$ and then step uphill to $\theta_{k+1}$.

So the “performance/objective surface” is just the plot of expected return vs.\ $\theta$.  Each update is you moving to a new point on that surface where you hope the expected return is higher.
