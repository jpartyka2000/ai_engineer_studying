Exactly—when you call:

```python
y_pred_proba = cross_val_predict(
    model,
    closed_df,
    series_target,
    cv=cv,
    method="predict_proba"
)[:, 1]
```

what happens under the hood is:

1. `cv.split(closed_df, series_target)` generates your (train_idx, test_idx) pairs.
2. For each fold, `cross_val_predict`  
   a. Fits `model` on the **train** indices (everything _except_ the current test fold).  
   b. Calls `predict_proba` on the **test** indices (the hold‑out fold).  
   c. Writes those test‑fold predictions into the corresponding slots of the output array.
3. After cycling through all folds, you end up with a probability for **every** sample—each one coming from the model that never saw that sample in its training set.

So yes: the resulting `y_pred_proba[i]` is the probability assigned to instance _i_ when it was held out as the “test” in its fold. You never get a prediction on a sample from a model that was trained on that same sample.
