That “1.515” you’re seeing isn’t the $p_s - p$ term from WRAcc (which, as we showed, can never exceed 1). What pysubgroup calls **lift** in the printed table is actually the **ratio** of subgroup precision to baseline precision:

$$
\text{lift (ratio)} \;=\; \frac{p_s}{p}
$$

—where

* $p_s$ = positives\_in\_subgroup / subgroup\_size
* $p$ = positives\_in\_dataset / total\_size

A ratio‐lift of 1.515 simply means your subgroup’s positive rate is 1.515 × higher than the global rate.

If you want to see the **absolute difference** $(p_s - p)$ that goes into WRAcc, look instead at the **quality** (WRAcc) column in your `df_rules` (or compute it yourself from the subgroup support and positives). That difference can never exceed 1, but the **ratio** can be any value ≥ 0 (and will be > 1 exactly when the subgroup is “purer” than the dataset as a whole).
