Yes—whatever you passed in as your `qf` (here, `ps.WRAccQF()`) is exactly what gets reported in the `quality` column of your `df_rules`. In other words:

* **`quality` = WRAcc**
* And since you chose WRAcc as your quality function, the numbers you see under “quality” are the weighted-relative-accuracy scores, i.e.

  $$
    \mathrm{WRAcc}(S)
    = \frac{|S|}{N}\;\times\;\bigl(p_s - p\bigr),
  $$

  where

  * $|S|$ is the subgroup size,
  * $N$ is the total dataset size,
  * $p_s$ is the subgroup’s positive rate,
  * $p$ is the overall positive rate.

All other columns in the output (support, size, lift, etc.) are derived statistics, but **“quality”** is the direct output of your chosen `qf`. If you had used, say, an Information‐Gain QF, then `quality` would show information gain instead.
