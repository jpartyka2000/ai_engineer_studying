To fill all blank cells in an Excel spreadsheet with 0, you can use the "Go To Special" feature. Here’s how:

1. **Select the Range:**  
   Select the range where you want to fill blank cells. To select the entire worksheet, click the triangle at the top-left corner (between row numbers and column letters) or press **Ctrl+A**.

2. **Open Go To Special:**  
   Press **F5** (or **Ctrl+G**) to open the "Go To" dialog box, then click on **Special…**.

3. **Select Blanks:**  
   In the "Go To Special" dialog, select **Blanks** and click **OK**. This will highlight all blank cells in the selected range.

4. **Fill with 0:**  
   With all blank cells selected, simply type **0**.

5. **Apply to All Selected Cells:**  
   Press **Ctrl+Enter**. This fills every highlighted (blank) cell with 0.

This method quickly populates every blank cell in your chosen range with the number 0.
