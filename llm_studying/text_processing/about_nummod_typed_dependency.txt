In dependency parsing, **`nummod`** stands for **"numeric modifier"** - it's a typed dependency that links a number to the noun it modifies.

## Definition:
`nummod` connects a numeric word that modifies the quantity or count of a nominal (noun) head.

## Examples:

**"I bought three books"**
```
books --nummod--> three
```
- "three" modifies "books" (tells us how many)

**"Sam ate 2 pizzas"**
```
pizzas --nummod--> 2
```
- "2" modifies "pizzas"

**"The price is 50 dollars"**
```
dollars --nummod--> 50
```
- "50" modifies "dollars"

## Key Characteristics:
- **Head:** The noun being quantified (books, pizzas, dollars)
- **Dependent:** The number (three, 2, 50)
- **Direction:** Arrow points FROM the noun TO the number

## What Counts as `nummod`:
- Cardinal numbers: "two", "5", "hundred"
- Arabic numerals: 1, 2, 100
- Number words: "dozen", "couple" (when used numerically)

## NOT `nummod`:
- Ordinals like "first", "second" → typically `amod` (adjectival modifier)
- Quantifiers like "many", "several" → typically `det` or `amod`
- Dates/times in full expressions → may use other relations

## Usage in NLP:
- **Information Extraction:** Extract quantities and counts
- **Question Answering:** Answer "how many?" questions  
- **Entity Recognition:** Identify numerical information
- **Text-to-data:** Convert text to structured data

This is part of the Universal Dependencies (UD) framework used by most modern dependency parsers like spaCy, Stanford Parser, etc.
