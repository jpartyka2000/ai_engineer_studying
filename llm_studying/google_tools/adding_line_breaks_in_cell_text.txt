Whenever you’re editing a cell (either by double‑clicking it or by pressing F2), you can insert a hard line break at your cursor via your keyboard:

* **Windows / Chrome OS**: Press **Alt + Enter**
* **Mac**: Press **Ctrl + Option + Enter**

That will split the cell’s text onto a new line.

---

### Make sure “Wrap” is on

After you add line breaks, you’ll want the cell to actually show them:

1. Select the cell (or range).
2. Click **Format → Text wrapping → Wrap**.

---

### Adding line breaks in formulas

If you’re building text via a formula, use `CHAR(10)` to insert a newline:

```plaintext
= "First line" & CHAR(10) & "Second line" & CHAR(10) & "Third line"
```

Again, just be sure Wrap is enabled on that cell so you see all the lines.
