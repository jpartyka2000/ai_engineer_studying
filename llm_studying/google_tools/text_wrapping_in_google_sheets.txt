In Google Sheets the default behavior is for text to overflow into empty neighboring cells rather than wrap. If yours is wrapping (or getting clipped), you can change it back to “overflow” like this:

1. **Select the cell or range** you want to change.
2. In the toolbar, click the **Text wrapping** icon (it looks like a ⤢ over lines).
3. Choose **Overflow** (the icon with an arrow pointing right).

Any text that’s longer than the column width will now spill into the next cell—just like a normal spreadsheet.

---

### Other quick tricks

* **Adjust column width**
  Hover over the right edge of the column header (e.g. between A and B), then double‑click to auto‑fit the widest entry.

* **See the full text in the formula bar**
  Click on a cell (or press **F2**) and read/edit the entire contents up top.

* **Temporarily wrap only for viewing**
  If you’d rather see it on multiple lines but don’t want to change column width, choose **Wrap** from that same Text wrapping menu.
