That's mostly correct—the SHAP values are in dollars because your model predictions are in dollars. However, it's important to be precise. The x‑axis in the summary plot represents the contribution of each feature, i.e., how much that feature pushes the prediction up or down relative to the baseline (the expected value).

So instead of labeling the axis simply as "($CLV)", a more accurate label would be something like:

- "Change in CLV ($)"
- "SHAP Value ($)"
- "Impact on CLV ($)"

These labels emphasize that the axis represents the feature's contribution (or shift) in dollar terms, not the final predicted CLV itself.
