Explainability is often treated as a post‑processing task—meaning that after a model is trained and making predictions, you use tools like SHAP, LIME, or other methods to analyze and explain its behavior. This is especially common for complex, black‑box models such as deep neural networks or ensemble methods where the model itself isn't inherently interpretable.

That said, explainability doesn't have to be entirely post‑hoc. There is also research and practice in building interpretable models from the start (like linear models or decision trees), and some approaches even integrate interpretability into the training process.

In summary, while many explainability techniques are applied after the model is built (post‑processing), there are also methods and models that are designed to be interpretable by design.
