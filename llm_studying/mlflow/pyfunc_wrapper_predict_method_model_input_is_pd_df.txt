Under the default **pyfunc** flavor, MLflow will always marshal your JSON or Pandas/Spark input into a **`pandas.DataFrame`** when calling your `predict()` method.  So your signature

```python
def predict(self, context, model_input: pd.DataFrame) -> pd.DataFrame:
    …
```

is exactly what you need: MLflow will hand you a DataFrame of the request payload.

While you can also log additional flavors (e.g. a Spark UDF), or re-serialize to a NumPy array in a custom wrapper, the **pyfunc** contract guarantees that `model_input` is a pandas DataFrame (or a list/dict that you convert yourself).  If you drop the DataFrame type your endpoint won’t receive correctly parsed inputs.
