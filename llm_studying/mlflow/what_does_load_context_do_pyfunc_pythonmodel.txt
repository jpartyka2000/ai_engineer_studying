The `load_context()` hook is invoked **once**, at model‐load time—i.e. when MLflow spins up your PythonModel (either via `mlflow.pyfunc.load_model(...)` in a client, or when the Model Serving container starts). At that moment MLflow hands you a `context` object (pointing at your logged artifacts directory), and you can use it to load any side files or parameters you logged alongside the model.

After `load_context()` finishes, MLflow will call your `predict()` any time it needs to score data. So:

* **Not** on every single `predict` call
* **Only once** per model instantiation, before the first `predict`
* In a served endpoint, that typically means once when the server starts up or a new worker process is forked

If you don’t need to pull in any extra artifacts, you can safely omit `load_context()` altogether.
