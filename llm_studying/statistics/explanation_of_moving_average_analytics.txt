# Explanation of `ma_*` Features

These features are derived from a **moving average** of time intervals between notes, designed to capture temporal patterns in musical performance or similar sequential data.

## Feature Breakdown

### 1. `ma_trend_slope`
**What it measures:** The overall direction and rate of change in the moving average over time.

A **positive slope** means intervals are getting longer (notes slowing down), while a **negative slope** means intervals are shrinking (notes speeding up). Calculated via linear regression on the moving average values.

### 2. `ma_variance`
**What it measures:** How much the moving average fluctuates.

**Low variance** indicates consistent, steady timing. **High variance** suggests erratic or highly variable tempo changes throughout the sequence.

### 3. `ma_last_value`
**What it measures:** The most recent smoothed interval value.

This captures the "current state" of timing at the end of the sequence—useful for understanding where the tempo ended up, regardless of how it got there.

### 4. `ma_acceleration`
**What it measures:** Whether the rate of change itself is changing (second-order trend).

Computed by comparing the average of the first half to the second half. **Positive acceleration** means the sequence is slowing down more in the second half. **Negative acceleration** means it's speeding up more toward the end.

### 5. `ma_ratio_last_to_first`
**What it measures:** The proportional change from start to finish.

A ratio of **1.0** means no overall change. Values **> 1** indicate the final intervals are longer than initial ones (overall deceleration). Values **< 1** indicate overall acceleration.

---

These features collectively transform a variable-length time series into a fixed set of scalars suitable for machine learning models, capturing trend, variability, recency, and acceleration dynamics.
