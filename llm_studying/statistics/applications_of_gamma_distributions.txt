The **gamma distribution** is typically used to model **waiting times, lifetimes, and count-based processes**. Some common applications include:  

### **1. Waiting Times & Reliability Analysis**
- Models the time until an event occurs **multiple times** (e.g., the sum of multiple independent exponential waiting times).  
- Example: The time until a machine fails after multiple small repairs.  

### **2. Queuing Theory & Service Times**
- Used to model service times in queuing systems (e.g., call centers, hospital services).  
- Example: The time a customer spends in a system with multiple service phases.  

### **3. Insurance & Risk Management**
- Models claim sizes and aggregate losses in insurance.  
- Example: The distribution of total claims within a given period.  

### **4. Bayesian Statistics (Prior Distributions)**
- The **gamma distribution** is often used as a **prior** for the rate parameter (*λ*) of an **exponential** or **Poisson** distribution.  
- Example: Estimating the rate of email arrivals per minute in Bayesian inference.  

### **5. Biological and Environmental Data**
- Models rainfall amounts, species survival times, or tumor growth rates.
