## Why This Design Is Nice

It lets users think in **relative importance** rather than fractions:

```python
# Intuitive: "MI is 3x as important as redundancy"
("mutual_information", 3.0),
("redundancy_score", 1.0),

# Equivalent but less intuitive:
("mutual_information", 0.75),
("redundancy_score", 0.25),
```

You can also use simple ratios like `(2.0, 1.0, 1.0)` or even `(6.0, 3.0, 1.0)` without worrying about the math—the normalization handles it.
