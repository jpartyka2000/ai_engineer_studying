A smoothness score of **0.85** (using  
\[
\text{smoothness} = \frac{1}{1 + \mathrm{std}(\Delta)},
\]
with \(\Delta_i = \text{obs}_{i+1}-\text{obs}_i\)) sits in the “good but not perfect” zone.  As a reminder:

- **1.00** ⇒ perfectly even rises (ideal)  
- **0.90–1.00** ⇒ very smooth, only tiny kinks  
- **0.80–0.90** ⇒ generally smooth with minor irregularities  
- **<0.80** ⇒ noticeable unevenness or dips/jumps  

So at **0.85** you’ve got a mostly monotonic, gently‐sloping calibration curve—slight deviations here and there, but nothing alarming.  You’d only start to worry if you dipped below 0.80, where the quartile‐to‐quartile jumps become large enough to suggest real rank‐ordering problems.
