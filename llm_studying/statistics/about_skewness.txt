## Skewness

**Skewness** measures the asymmetry of a probability distribution around its mean. It tells you whether the data leans to one side, with a longer tail extending in one direction.

The most common formula (Fisher's moment coefficient of skewness) is:

$$\text{Skewness} = \frac{E[(X - \mu)^3]}{\sigma^3}$$

This is the third standardized moment of the distribution.

**Interpreting values:**
- **Skewness = 0:** Symmetric distribution (like a normal distribution)
- **Skewness > 0 (positive):** Right-skewed—tail extends to the right, with most values concentrated on the left (e.g., income distributions)
- **Skewness < 0 (negative):** Left-skewed—tail extends to the left, with most values concentrated on the right (e.g., age at retirement)

## How skewness differs from CV

| Aspect | Coefficient of Variation | Skewness |
|--------|--------------------------|----------|
| **What it measures** | Relative spread/dispersion | Asymmetry of shape |
| **Mathematical basis** | Ratio of std dev to mean (1st and 2nd moments) | Third standardized moment |
| **Question it answers** | "How much variation relative to the average?" | "Is the distribution lopsided?" |
| **Symmetry dependence** | A symmetric distribution can have any CV | A symmetric distribution always has skewness ≈ 0 |
| **Sign interpretation** | Always positive (for positive data) | Sign indicates direction of the tail |

## A concrete example

Consider two datasets:
- **Dataset A:** values tightly clustered but with a few extreme outliers on the right
- **Dataset B:** values spread widely but symmetrically

Dataset A might have low CV (most values near the mean) but high positive skewness (that right tail). Dataset B might have high CV (wide spread) but zero skewness (symmetric).

## Relevance to ML

Skewness matters for ML because many algorithms assume or perform better with roughly symmetric, normally-distributed features. Highly skewed features can cause issues with models sensitive to outliers (like linear regression) or distance-based methods. Common remedies include log transforms, Box-Cox transforms, or quantile transformations to reduce skewness before training.
