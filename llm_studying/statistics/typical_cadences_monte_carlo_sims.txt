The cadence really depends on the business domain and the purpose of the simulation. Here are some common patterns:

1. **Real‐Time or Near-Real-Time**

   * **Use Case:** High-frequency trading, intra-day risk dashboards, live pricing engines.
   * **Frequency:** Continuously (streaming), or every few seconds/minutes via a persistent service.

2. **Daily**

   * **Use Case:** End-of-day Value-at-Risk (VaR) for portfolios, retail demand forecasting refresh, “overnight” batch scoring.
   * **Frequency:** Once per trading day (e.g. run after market close), or nightly in non-finance settings.

3. **Weekly**

   * **Use Case:** Inventory re-order simulations, weekly campaign budget re-allocations, supply-chain “what-if” scenarios.
   * **Frequency:** Every Monday (or business-week cadence) to reset planning inputs.

4. **Monthly**

   * **Use Case:** Budget planning, marketing spend MC (e.g. “what if we shift next month’s budget”), P\&L forecasting, resource capacity planning.
   * **Frequency:** Once per month, often aligned to the start or end of the month.

5. **Quarterly or Annual**

   * **Use Case:** Strategic financial planning, capital-expenditure scenario analysis, long-term actuarial projections.
   * **Frequency:** Quarterly board packages or once per year during budgeting season.

---

### How to pick your cadence

* **Data availability & stability:** If your input data (prices, clicks, conversions) only updates nightly, there’s little benefit to more frequent runs.
* **Decision rhythms:** Align your MC runs to when decisions get made (daily risk committees, weekly marketing stand-ups, monthly finance closes).
* **Compute cost vs. timeliness:** Large, full-scale MC runs can take hours—if you need sub-hour turn-around, you may opt for smaller, targeted simulations more often.
* **Regulatory or SLA requirements:** In regulated finance, some VaR or stress-test simulations must run daily (or even intraday) to meet compliance.

In practice you often see a **“fast lane”** (lightweight, low-latency MC for ad-hoc queries) running continuously, plus a **“batch lane”** (full-scale simulations overnight or monthly) that produces heavy-duty reports. Choose the rhythm that matches both your **data refresh rate** and your **stakeholder decision cycle**.
