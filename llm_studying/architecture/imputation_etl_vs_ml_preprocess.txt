It's generally more appropriate to handle missing value imputation in the ML preprocessing pipeline rather than in the ETL pipeline. Here's why:

### Reasons to Impute in the ML Preprocessing Pipeline
1. **Data Distribution Awareness**: The ML preprocessing step often has access to more context about the data, such as understanding feature distributions, correlations, or relationships between features. This allows for more sophisticated imputation methods (e.g., mean, median, mode, KNN, or model-based imputations).

2. **Flexibility**: The imputation strategy may vary depending on the ML model you're using. For example, some models might perform better with median imputation, while others might benefit from more advanced techniques. Keeping this step in the ML preprocessing pipeline ensures you can tailor the imputation strategy to the specific model.

3. **Consistency Across Training and Prediction**: By performing imputation in the ML preprocessing pipeline, you ensure that the same strategy is applied consistently during both training and prediction phases.

### When to Impute in the ETL Pipeline
You might choose to handle basic imputations in the ETL pipeline if:
- Certain columns are essential for downstream data integrity or analysis, and nulls would cause issues in subsequent transformations.
- You need to fill nulls with default or placeholder values (e.g., 0 for numerical columns or "Unknown" for categorical columns) to maintain data completeness early on.

However, leaving imputation to the ML preprocessing stage generally offers more flexibility and accuracy for building your model.
