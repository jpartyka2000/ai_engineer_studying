There isn’t a universal “maximum” number that applies in every scenario. Instead, you should tune the number of shuffle partitions based on your workload and cluster resources. Here are some guidelines:

- **Default Setting:** Spark’s default is usually 200, which works well for many moderate workloads.
- **Partition Size:** A common guideline is to aim for a partition size between 100 MB and 1 GB after shuffle. If partitions are much smaller than this, you might reduce the number of partitions to lower overhead; if they’re much larger, increasing the number can improve parallelism.
- **Cluster Resources:** For clusters with many cores and ample memory, a higher number of partitions can help maximize parallelism. For smaller clusters, a lower number might be better.
- **Empirical Tuning:** Monitor your job’s performance and adjust as needed. There’s no one-size-fits-all number.

In summary, rather than a strict maximum, focus on ensuring that each partition is appropriately sized for your data and cluster. Experiment with different values and use Spark’s UI or metrics to determine what works best for your workload.
