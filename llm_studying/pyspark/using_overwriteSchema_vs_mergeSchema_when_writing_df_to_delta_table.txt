That's the issue right there. Since you're using `mergeSchema=true`, Delta is **preserving** the existing table schema and merging in any new columns from your DataFrame. Those 6 columns exist in the table's schema from previous runs, so they persist with NULLs even though your DataFrame no longer has them.

## The Fix

Since you're already doing a full `overwrite`, just swap `mergeSchema` for `overwriteSchema`:

```python
final_df_spark.write.format("delta").mode("overwrite").option(
    "overwriteSchema", "true"
).saveAsTable(OUTPUT_TABLE_NAME)
```

**What this does:** On every write, the table schema will exactly match your DataFrame's schema. Those 6 columns will be gone on the next run, and if your predictors change in the future, the schema will stay in sync automatically.

## Quick Comparison

| Option | Behavior |
|--------|----------|
| `mergeSchema=true` | Keeps all existing columns + adds new ones (columns accumulate) |
| `overwriteSchema=true` | Replaces schema entirely with DataFrame's schema |

Since you're overwriting all the data anyway, `overwriteSchema` is the right choice here—it ensures your table always reflects exactly what your pipeline produces.
