Spark DataFrames are immutable and lazily evaluated objects that represent a logical plan (a Directed Acyclic Graph, or DAG) of transformations rather than an in-memory collection of data. When you assign a DataFrame to another variable, you’re not creating a traditional shallow or deep copy. Instead, you're simply creating another reference to the same immutable logical plan. Any transformation you apply results in a new DataFrame (i.e., a new logical plan) that doesn't alter the original. 

So, it's best to think of Spark DataFrames as immutable objects with a DAG of transformations. They don't follow the typical "copy" semantics because no actual data is duplicated in memory until an action is triggered.
