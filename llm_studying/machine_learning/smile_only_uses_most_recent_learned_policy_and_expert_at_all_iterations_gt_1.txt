## Small Correction

### SMILE: Blends with Only ONE Policy Per Iteration

At iteration i, SMILE blends:
- **Expert policy π*** 
- **Current learned policy π(i)** (just the most recent one, not multiple prior policies)

So at iteration 5:
- SMILE mixes π* and π(5) only
- Not π(1), π(2), π(3), π(4) - just π(5)

### Why?

Each iteration trains a new policy that's supposed to be better than the last. So you always use the most recent/best learned policy for mixing, not older ones.

## Summary

**DAgger at iteration i:**
- Trajectories come purely from π(i)

**SMILE at iteration i:**
- Trajectories come from stochastic mixture of π* and π(i)
- (Not π* + π(1) + π(2) + ... + π(i), just π* + π(i))

That's the only refinement - otherwise you've nailed the core difference! Each algorithm uses only the current learned policy, just in different ways.
