Yes, it can, but it depends on how you define the "duration" and "event" for your survival analysis. In a Kaplan-Meier survival curve for subscriptions, each customer is represented by:

- **Duration:** The time (e.g., days, months) from when they started their subscription until either they churn (an event) or the data is censored (they're still active).
- **Event Indicator:** A binary flag (1 or 0) indicating whether the event (e.g., churn) has occurred.

The Kaplan-Meier method doesn’t require multiple transactions per customer. Even customers with just 1 transaction—or even those with 0 transactions—can be included as long as you have a meaningful "duration" for them. For example:

- **1 Transaction:** A customer who made one transaction might have a duration from subscription start to either churn or the current date if still active.
- **0 Transactions:** A customer who has never transacted may still have a recorded subscription start date. In such cases, you can use the time from sign-up to the present as the duration and mark them as censored (event indicator 0) if they haven’t churned.

In summary, as long as you have defined what “duration” means in your context (e.g., time from sign-up to churn/censoring) and correctly indicate whether the event occurred, the Kaplan-Meier method will work regardless of the number of transactions.
