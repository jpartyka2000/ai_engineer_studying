Including the sales cycle type (monthly, quarterly, annual) in your CLV regression model can be very beneficial if it captures differences in customer behavior and revenue patterns. For example:

- **Different Revenue Streams:** Customers on a monthly cycle might generate smaller, more frequent payments compared to those on an annual cycle, which can affect overall CLV.
- **Retention and Churn Patterns:** The sales cycle could correlate with customer retention, where the length of the cycle might reflect different engagement levels.
- **Cost Structures:** Billing frequency might also influence operational costs or discounts offered, further impacting CLV.

In short, if the sales cycle type influences how revenue is generated and how customers behave over time, it’s a valuable feature to consider. You can also use explainability tools like SHAP to verify its impact on your predictions.
