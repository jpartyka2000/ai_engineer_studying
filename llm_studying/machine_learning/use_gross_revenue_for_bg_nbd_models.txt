Yes, you can use the subscription fee data (dollar amount per period) as your monetary value input for a BG/NBD plus Gamma-Gamma CLV model. Here's what to keep in mind:

- **BG/NBD Focus:**  
  The BG/NBD model itself only predicts the number of future transactions based on frequency, recency, and the observation period. It doesn't use monetary value.

- **Gamma-Gamma Component:**  
  When you combine BG/NBD with a Gamma-Gamma model to estimate CLV, the Gamma-Gamma model uses the average revenue per transaction. In your case, if you only have the subscription fee (without overhead costs), this will represent the gross revenue per transaction.

- **Gross vs. Net Revenue:**  
  Using the subscription fee gives you gross revenue estimates. If you later need net revenue or profit-based CLV, you'll have to adjust for costs or overheads.

So, in short, if you're comfortable working with gross revenue, then using your dollar amount per period is acceptable for building a BG/NBD + Gamma-Gamma CLV model.
