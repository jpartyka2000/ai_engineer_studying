Let’s break down that one line into its building blocks and parameters:

```python
result = ps.BeamSearch(
    task=ps.SubgroupDiscoveryTask(df, target, qf, depth=3),
    beam_width=5,
    result_set_size=10
).run()
```

---

## 1. `ps.SubgroupDiscoveryTask(df, target, qf, depth=3)`

This creates the **search problem** you want to solve.

* **`df`**
  Your input `pandas.DataFrame` containing all predictor columns plus whatever column(s) `target` refers to.

* **`target`**
  A `ps.BinaryTarget` (or `ps.NumericTarget`) object that tells PySubgroup:

  * which column in `df` is the outcome,
  * what value counts as “positive” (e.g. `value=1`).

* **`qf`** (quality function)
  A `ps.QualityFunction` instance (e.g. `ps.WRAccQF()`, `ps.CQF()`, etc.) that scores each candidate subgroup.

  * **WRAcc** = Weighted Relative Accuracy, balances coverage vs. precision.
  * You pick this based on whether you care more about high‐precision vs. large coverage, etc.

* **`depth=3`**
  The **maximum rule length** (i.e. the max number of selectors/conditions combined in one subgroup).

  * `depth=1` → single‐feature rules like `Age < 30`.
  * `depth=3` → up to three conditions AND‑ed together (e.g. `Age<30 AND Country='US' AND Income>50k`).

---

## 2. `ps.BeamSearch(..., beam_width=5, result_set_size=10)`

This configures **how** you search the space of possible rules.

* **`beam_width=5`**
  At each search level (i.e. each rule length), you **keep only the top 5 candidates** (by quality) to expand further.

  * A higher beam width explores more candidates (but costs more time).
  * A lower beam width runs faster but may miss good rules.

* **`result_set_size=10`**
  After the full search, return the **10 best subgroups** (ranked by your quality function).

  * If you ask for 10 but only 7 meet your minimum support, you’ll get fewer.

---

## 3. `.run()`

* **Executes** the beam‐search algorithm on your `SubgroupDiscoveryTask`.
* **Returns** a list (or `ResultSet`) of up to `result_set_size` `Subgroup` objects, each with attributes like:

  * `.subgroup_description` (the rule),
  * `.subgroup_size` (how many rows match),
  * `.cover` (how many of those are positive),
  * `.quality_` (the score).

---

### Putting it all together

1. **Define** your data, target, and scoring via `SubgroupDiscoveryTask`.
2. **Search** with a beam of width 5, allowing rules up to 3 conditions.
3. **Return** the top 10 subgroups.
4. **`.run()`** actually performs the computation and hands you the results in `result`.
