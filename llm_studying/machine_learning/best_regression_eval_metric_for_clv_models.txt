For a **Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) model**, the best evaluation metric depends on how you prioritize different types of prediction errors. Here’s a breakdown:  

### **1. Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE) → Best for CLV** ✅  
**Why?**  
- CLV predictions can have large variability, and RMSE **penalizes large errors more heavily**.  
- Since CLV values are in monetary units, RMSE is easier to interpret in real-world terms.  
- Helps prevent large overestimations, which can lead to costly business decisions.  

### **2. Mean Absolute Error (MAE) → Good if you care about average error**  
**Why?**  
- If you want to minimize the average dollar error per customer, MAE is useful.  
- Less sensitive to extreme outliers than RMSE.  
- A good choice if outliers exist but are **not business-critical**.  

### **3. Mean Squared Error (MSE) → Similar to RMSE but harder to interpret**  
**Why?**  
- Penalizes large errors even more than RMSE, but its squared units (dollars²) make it less interpretable.  
- **Use RMSE instead** since it has the same benefits but is in real-world units.  

### **4. R² Score → Not ideal** ❌  
**Why?**  
- R² measures **relative fit**, but in CLV, absolute error (in dollars) is more important.  
- A high R² doesn’t necessarily mean your revenue predictions are accurate.  

---

### **Which One Should You Use?**
✅ **RMSE** → If you want an interpretable metric and to penalize large errors.  
✅ **MAE** → If you care more about average error and have outliers.  
❌ **Avoid MSE** (harder to interpret) and **R²** (not the best for dollar predictions).
