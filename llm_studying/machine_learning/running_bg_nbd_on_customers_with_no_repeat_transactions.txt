The BG/NBD model is designed to provide predictions even for customers with only one transaction (i.e., frequency = 0), but there are some nuances:

- **Less Data, Greater Uncertainty:**  
  When a customer has made only one purchase, the model has very limited information about their repeat behavior. As a result, the prediction for future transactions is based mainly on the recency (time since the first purchase) and the observation period (T), which may lead to higher uncertainty or less reliable predictions for that individual.

- **Typically Lower Predictions:**  
  In many cases, the model will predict fewer future transactions for one-time customers compared to those with multiple transactions. This is consistent with the idea that customers who haven’t repeated their purchase are less likely to do so in the future.

- **Model Assumptions:**  
  The accuracy of the prediction for one-transaction customers depends on how well the underlying assumptions (e.g., a Poisson process for transactions and a Beta-Geometric dropout process) match your data. If those assumptions hold reasonably well, then the predictions, while less precise, are still informative.

In summary, while the BG/NBD model does generate predictions for customers with only one transaction, those predictions come with greater uncertainty and are generally lower. You might consider supplementing the model or using additional segmentation if these predictions are critical to your analysis.
