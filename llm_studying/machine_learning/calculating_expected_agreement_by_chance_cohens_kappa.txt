Great question! In **Cohen’s Kappa (κ)**, the **expected probability of agreement by chance** (\( P_e \)) is calculated based on the assumption that both raters are making independent random decisions.

### **Formula for Expected Agreement (\( P_e \))**
\[
P_e = (p_{yes,1} \times p_{yes,2}) + (p_{no,1} \times p_{no,2})
\]
where:  
- \( p_{yes,1} \) = proportion of times Rater 1 assigns **"Success"**  
- \( p_{no,1} \) = proportion of times Rater 1 assigns **"Failure"**  
- \( p_{yes,2} \) = proportion of times Rater 2 assigns **"Success"**  
- \( p_{no,2} \) = proportion of times Rater 2 assigns **"Failure"**  

### **Step-by-Step Example**
Imagine a dataset where two raters labeled 100 responses as either **Success (1)** or **Failure (0)**:

| Label  | Rater 1 Count | Rater 2 Count |
|--------|--------------|--------------|
| **Success (1)** | 70 | 60 |
| **Failure (0)** | 30 | 40 |
| **Total** | 100 | 100 |

Now, compute the expected probabilities:

\[
p_{yes,1} = \frac{70}{100} = 0.7, \quad p_{no,1} = \frac{30}{100} = 0.3
\]
\[
p_{yes,2} = \frac{60}{100} = 0.6, \quad p_{no,2} = \frac{40}{100} = 0.4
\]
\[
P_e = (0.7 \times 0.6) + (0.3 \times 0.4) = 0.42 + 0.12 = 0.54
\]

This means that **54% of agreement is expected by chance**. Cohen’s Kappa will then adjust for this chance agreement.
