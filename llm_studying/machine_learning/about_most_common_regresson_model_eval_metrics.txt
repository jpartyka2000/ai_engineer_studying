For regression models, the most popular evaluation metric depends on the specific use case, but the most widely used ones are:

### **1. Mean Squared Error (MSE)**
✅ **Formula:**  
\[
MSE = \frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^{n} (y_i - \hat{y}_i)^2
\]
✅ **When to Use:**  
- If large errors should be penalized more (due to the squaring).  
- Common in deep learning and theoretical applications.  

---

### **2. Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE)**
✅ **Formula:**  
\[
RMSE = \sqrt{MSE}
\]
✅ **When to Use:**  
- When interpretability is important (units match the target variable).  
- Similar to MSE but more intuitive.  

---

### **3. Mean Absolute Error (MAE)**
✅ **Formula:**  
\[
MAE = \frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^{n} |y_i - \hat{y}_i|
\]
✅ **When to Use:**  
- If **absolute differences** are preferred over squared differences.  
- Less sensitive to outliers than MSE/RMSE.  

---

### **4. R² Score (Coefficient of Determination)**
✅ **Formula:**  
\[
R^2 = 1 - \frac{SS_{res}}{SS_{tot}}
\]
✅ **When to Use:**  
- When you want a **normalized measure** of model fit (ranges from 0 to 1).  
- Higher values (closer to 1) mean better predictive ability.  

---

### **Which One Should You Use?**
- **MSE / RMSE** → If **penalizing large errors more heavily** is important.  
- **MAE** → If outliers exist and errors should be **treated equally**.  
- **R²** → If you need a **relative measure** of fit.
