Here’s what each index and symbol is tracking in the SAG formulas:

| Symbol                                                      | Subscript(s)    | Superscript(s)    | Meaning                                                                                                               |
| ----------------------------------------------------------- | --------------- | ----------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| $n$                                                         | —               | —                 | Total number of training samples.                                                                                     |
| $i\in\{1,\dots,n\}$                                         | Subscript       | —                 | Labels one particular sample in the dataset.                                                                          |
| $t\in\{0,1,2,\dots\}$                                       | —               | Superscript       | The iteration count (time step) of the algorithm. $t=0$ is “before any update,” $t=1$ is after the first update, etc. |
| $i_t$                                                       | Subscript       | —                 | The specific sample index chosen *at iteration* $t$.                                                                  |
| $w^{(t)}$                                                   | —               | Superscript       | The model’s weight vector *after* completing iteration $t$. (So $w^{(0)}$ is your initialization.)                    |
| $g_i^{(t)}$                                                 | Subscript       | Superscript       | The *stored* gradient for sample $i$, as of the end of iteration $t$.  Initially $g_i^{(0)}=0$.                       |
| $S^{(t)}$                                                   | —               | Superscript       | The running sum of **all** stored gradients at iteration $t$:                                                         |
| $\;S^{(t)}=\sum_{j=1}^n g_j^{(t)}$.  Initially $S^{(0)}=0$. |                 |                   |                                                                                                                       |
| $\nabla f_i(w)$                                             | Subscript       | —                 | The *true* gradient of the loss on sample $i$ evaluated at weights $w$.                                               |
| $g_{i_t}^{(t-1)}$                                           | Subscript $i_t$ | Superscript $t-1$ | “The old” stored gradient for the chosen sample $i_t$ *before* you overwrite it at iteration $t$.                     |
| $g_{i_t}^{(t)}$                                             | Subscript $i_t$ | Superscript $t$   | “The new” gradient you’ve just computed for sample $i_t$ at the current weights $w^{(t-1)}$.                          |

---

### How they fit into the update steps

1. **Pick** a sample index $i_t$.
2. **Compute** its fresh gradient at the *previous* weights:

   $$
     g_{i_t}^{(t)} \;=\;\nabla f_{i_t}\bigl(w^{(t-1)}\bigr).
   $$
3. **Update** the running sum by *removing* the old stored gradient for $i_t$ and *adding* the new one:

   $$
     S^{(t)} 
     = S^{(t-1)}
       \;-\; g_{i_t}^{(t-1)}
       \;+\; g_{i_t}^{(t)}.
   $$
4. **Step** the weights along the *average* gradient direction:

   $$
     w^{(t)}
     = w^{(t-1)}
       \;-\;\frac{\alpha}{n}\;S^{(t)}.
   $$

* The **superscript** always tells you *“which iteration”* that quantity refers to.
* The **subscript** on gradients $\;g_i^{(\cdot)}$ identifies *which sample’s* gradient is being stored.
* $S^{(t)}$ bundles up all $n$ of those $g_i^{(t)}$ into one sum for the update.

Once each sample has been visited at least once, every $g_i$ in the memory is “fresh,” so $S^{(t)}/n$ becomes the true average gradient—combining the cheap per‐step cost of SGD with the fast, variance‐reduced convergence of full‐batch methods.
