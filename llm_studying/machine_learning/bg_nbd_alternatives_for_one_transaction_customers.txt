You're right that **BG/NBD (Beta-Geometric/Negative Binomial Distribution)** and similar probabilistic models struggle when a customer has **only one transaction**, since they rely on repeat purchase behavior to estimate future transactions.  

### **Alternative Approaches for Predicting Transactions & Revenue for One-Time Buyers:**

---

### **1️⃣ Survival Analysis (Time-to-Event Modeling)**
- **Why?** It estimates when (or if) a customer will make a future purchase.
- **How?** Treat the second purchase as the "event" and time since the first purchase as the "observation period."
- **Models to Use:**
  - **Kaplan-Meier Estimator** (non-parametric, works for simple survival analysis)
  - **Cox Proportional Hazards Model** (assesses impact of features on purchase likelihood)
  - **Weibull or Gamma Parametric Models** (flexible for time-to-event prediction)

✅ **Good if:** You want to predict when a customer will purchase again, even if they have only one transaction.

---

### **2️⃣ Machine Learning Regression (Supervised Learning)**
- **Why?** If you have customer features (demographics, ad interactions, web behavior), a **regression model** can predict **future purchase count** and **spend**.  
- **How?** Train a model using features of customers with multiple transactions to predict purchases/revenue for single-purchase users.  
- **Models to Try:**  
  - **Gradient Boosting Models (e.g., XGBoost, LightGBM, CatBoost)** – Handle tabular data well.  
  - **Random Forest Regressor** – Can model nonlinear relationships.  
  - **Neural Networks** – If you have a large dataset with complex interactions.  
  - **GLM (Poisson or Negative Binomial Regression)** – Good for count data like transactions.  

✅ **Good if:** You have detailed **customer data** and want to leverage ML for prediction.

---

### **3️⃣ Similarity-Based Methods (Nearest Neighbors)**
- **Why?** Find past customers who looked similar (but had more transactions) and use their patterns to estimate behavior.
- **How?** Use **K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN)** or **clustering (e.g., K-Means, DBSCAN)** to identify **lookalike** customers based on their first transaction and profile.
- **Example:**  
  - Find the **most similar customers** who had multiple purchases.  
  - Use their **average future transactions/revenue** as an estimate for the one-time buyer.  

✅ **Good if:** You have many customers and want a **data-driven, non-parametric** approach.

---

### **4️⃣ Hierarchical Bayesian Models**
- **Why?** Can model uncertainty better than traditional ML models.
- **How?** Use **Hierarchical Poisson-Gamma or Hierarchical Bayesian Regression** to estimate transactions.
- **Key Idea:**  
  - If a customer has only 1 transaction, Bayesian priors can be inferred from similar customers.  
  - Helps in cases of **data sparsity**.

✅ **Good if:** You want to incorporate **uncertainty** and **prior knowledge** into your model.

---

### **5️⃣ Customer Segmentation + Heuristic Rules**
- **Why?** Sometimes a **simple rule-based approach** can work well.
- **How?**  
  - Segment customers into **cohorts** based on first purchase behavior (spend, channel, product type).  
  - Calculate **average purchase rates** within each segment.  
  - Apply these estimates to new customers in the same segment.

✅ **Good if:** You want an **interpretable** model without complex ML.

---

### **Which Should You Choose?**
| Approach | Best When… | Complexity | Assumptions |
|----------|------------|------------|-------------|
| **Survival Analysis** | You want to predict *when* a customer will buy again | Medium | Assumes time-to-event patterns exist |
| **ML Regression** | You have customer features & want accurate numerical predictions | High | Needs enough training data |
| **Similarity-Based (KNN/Clustering)** | You lack labeled data but have many customers | Low-Medium | Assumes customers with similar first transactions behave alike |
| **Hierarchical Bayesian Models** | You want to incorporate uncertainty | High | Requires statistical expertise |
| **Segmentation + Heuristics** | You want a simple, interpretable method | Low | Assumes past behavior generalizes |

🚀 **Best Bet for Your Case:**  
- If you have **rich customer data**, use **ML regression (XGBoost/Poisson GLM)**.  
- If you want an **interpretable rule-based** approach, use **segmentation + heuristics**.  
- If you want to model **repeat purchases over time**, try **Survival Analysis**.
