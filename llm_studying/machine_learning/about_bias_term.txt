Absolutely — here’s a concise overview of the bias term in logistic regression:


---

🎯 What is the Bias Term?

The bias (usually denoted as b) is a scalar value added to the weighted sum of inputs:

z = \mathbf{w}^\top \mathbf{x} + b

It acts like the intercept in linear regression.


---

🧠 Why is the Bias Important?

It shifts the decision boundary left or right, independent of the input features.

Without it, the model would be forced to pass through the origin — which can severely limit its flexibility.



---

🔄 How is it Updated During Training?

During gradient descent, we compute:

\text{db} = \frac{1}{n} \sum_i (\hat{y}_i - y_i)

This is the average prediction error across all examples. We then adjust b in the opposite direction of this error to improve the fit.


---

🧭 Intuition

Imagine the bias as a "baseline" prediction. It helps the model predict correctly even when all feature values are 0.
