Here are a few additional sales-rep-based win rate metrics that you can engineer using as few other features as possible:

### 1. **Rep Win Rate Over Last N Days**
   - Calculate the win rate for each sales rep based on opportunities closed in the last N days (e.g., 30, 60, or 90 days).
   - This metric captures recent performance trends while smoothing out short-term fluctuations.

   **How to Calculate**:
   - Filter opportunities closed within the past N days for each rep.
   - Compute the win rate as the ratio of won opportunities to total closed opportunities within this period.

### 2. **Rep Average Time to Win**
   - Measure the average time taken by a sales rep to win an opportunity. This metric helps identify reps who close deals faster.
   - Can be calculated by taking the average duration (e.g., days) between `deal_created_date` and `deal_closed_date` for won opportunities.

### 3. **Rep Consistency Win Rate**
   - Compute the win rate for each sales rep for each time interval (e.g., monthly or quarterly) and then calculate the standard deviation across these intervals.
   - A lower standard deviation indicates more consistent performance over time, while a higher value indicates inconsistency.

### 4. **Rep Win Rate for High-Value Deals**
   - Calculate the win rate for opportunities above a certain threshold (e.g., deals with an `amount` greater than $10,000).
   - This metric indicates how well a rep performs when handling high-value deals.

### 5. **Rep Conversion Rate by Stage**
   - Identify the win rate for each rep based on different opportunity stages (e.g., from "Proposal" to "Won"). This helps determine which reps excel at moving deals through specific stages.

### 6. **Rep First Attempt Win Rate**
   - Calculate the win rate based only on the first opportunities handled by each sales rep (e.g., first 5 or 10 opportunities).
   - This metric gives insights into a rep's early effectiveness or learning curve.

### 7. **Win Rate Momentum**
   - Measure changes in win rate over recent time periods (e.g., comparing win rates of the last 3 months to the previous 3 months).
   - Helps assess whether a rep's performance is improving or declining.

These metrics provide diverse perspectives on sales rep performance and can offer valuable insights into strengths, weaknesses, and trends in their win rates. You can choose the ones most relevant to your analysis or business goals.
