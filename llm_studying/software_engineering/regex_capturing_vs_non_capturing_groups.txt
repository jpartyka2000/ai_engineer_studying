The main difference between a **capturing group** and a **non-capturing group** in regular expressions lies in whether the group stores the matched content for later use.

---

### **Capturing Group**
A **capturing group** is denoted by parentheses `()` and captures the part of the string matched by the enclosed pattern. This captured content is stored in memory and can be:
1. **Referenced in backreferences** within the regex.
2. **Retrieved programmatically** after the regex operation.

#### Syntax:
```regex
(pattern)
```

#### Use Case:
- Extracting or reusing matched content.

#### Example:
Regex: `(\d{4})-(\d{2})-(\d{2})`

- Input: `2024-12-27`
- Matches:
  - Group 1: `2024`
  - Group 2: `12`
  - Group 3: `27`
- You can use these groups in substitutions or processing.

---

### **Non-Capturing Group**
A **non-capturing group** is denoted by `(?:...)`. It groups the enclosed pattern for applying quantifiers or logical operations, but it **does not capture the matched content** or store it in memory.

#### Syntax:
```regex
(?:pattern)
```

#### Use Case:
- When you need grouping only for logic or quantifiers but don't need to store the matched content.

#### Example:
Regex: `(?:foo|bar)\d+`

- Input: `foo123 bar456`
- Matches: `foo123`, `bar456`
- **No groups are captured**—the matches are treated as a whole.

---

### Comparison Table

| Feature                     | Capturing Group `()`            | Non-Capturing Group `(?:...)`     |
|-----------------------------|----------------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| **Captures Matched Content** | Yes                             | No                                |
| **Usage in Backreferences**  | Supported                       | Not Supported                     |
| **Performance**              | Slightly slower (stores memory) | Slightly faster                   |
| **Primary Purpose**          | Extracting or reusing content   | Grouping without capturing        |

---

### Practical Examples

#### 1. Extracting vs. Skipping Data
Regex: `(\d{4})-(?:\d{2})-(\d{2})`

- Input: `2024-12-27`
- Captured Groups:
  - Group 1: `2024`
  - Group 2: `27`
- Explanation:
  - `(\d{4})`: Captures the year.
  - `(?:\d{2})`: Groups the month (but does not capture it).
  - `(\d{2})`: Captures the day.

#### 2. Applying Quantifiers
Regex: `(?:ha)+`

- Input: `hahaha`
- Matches: `hahaha`
- Explanation:
  - Groups `ha` for the quantifier `+` to match one or more instances, but no capturing occurs.

---

### When to Use Each
- **Use Capturing Groups** when you need to:
  - Extract parts of a match for further processing.
  - Create backreferences within the same regex or in replacements.
- **Use Non-Capturing Groups** when you:
  - Don't need to capture content.
  - Want to optimize performance or improve readability.
