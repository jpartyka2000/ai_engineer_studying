**Telemetry** is the collection and transmission of raw operational data from your systems—logs, metrics, traces, events, and health signals—into a centralized pipeline or backend. It’s the **plumbing** that gathers and ships data off your services so you can store, process, and analyze it.

**Observability** is the practice and ability to **interpret** that telemetry to understand your system’s internal state and behavior. An observable system is one where the collected telemetry (when properly instrumented and correlated) lets you answer questions like “Why did latency spike in production?” or “What caused that error rate increase?” without having to add more instrumentation on the fly.

In short:

* **Telemetry** = the data (logs, metrics, traces) + the pipeline that delivers it
* **Observability** = the capability to draw insights, detect anomalies, and troubleshoot using that telemetry
