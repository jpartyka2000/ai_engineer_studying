Yes—Black always makes sure your file ends with exactly one newline (a “trailing newline”). If you’re missing it, Black will add it; if you have extra blank lines at the end, Black will collapse them so there’s just a single `\n`.

Under the hood it also uses LF line endings by default (you can tweak that with the `--line-ending` option), but you’ll never end up with no newline at EOF or with a bunch of empty lines after your last bit of code.
