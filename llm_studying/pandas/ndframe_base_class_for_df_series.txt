### Vectorization in Pandas: No Dedicated ‚ÄúVectorized‚Äù Class

Pandas doesn‚Äôt introduce a special `Vectorized` or `Vectorizable` class to mark objects as vectorized. Instead, vectorized behavior is baked into its core abstractions:

---

#### 1. **`NDFrame`**

* **What it is:** The abstract base class for both **`Series`** and **`DataFrame`**
* **Why it matters:** All vectorized arithmetic (`+`, `-`, `*`, `/`, etc.), boolean masking, and ufunc support are defined here.
* **How to spot it:**

  ```python
  from pandas.core.generic import NDFrame
  isinstance(pd.Series([1,2,3]), NDFrame)     # True
  isinstance(pd.DataFrame({'A':[1,2]}), NDFrame)  # True
  ```

#### 2. **`IndexOpsMixin`**

* **What it is:** A mixin that implements the operator methods (`__add__`, `__eq__`, etc.) for **`Series`**, **`DataFrame`**, and **`Index`**
* **Why it matters:** It ensures that expressions like `df + 5` or `s > 0` are applied element‚Äëwise.

---

#### 3. Under the Hood: NumPy Arrays

* Every `Series` and the underlying blocks of a `DataFrame` store data in **NumPy `ndarray`s** (or an ExtensionArray interface).
* The heavy lifting of vectorization (broadcasting, ufuncs) is actually done by NumPy.

---

### What This Means for You

* **Anything** that subclasses `NDFrame` (i.e., `Series` or `DataFrame`) supports vectorized operations out of the box.
* You don‚Äôt need to look for or instantiate a special ‚Äúvectorized‚Äù class‚Äîjust work with `Series` and `DataFrame`, and leverage their methods (or the operators) for element‚Äëwise, fast computations.
