Yes—once you have your `GroupBy` object, you can inspect its `.groups` or `.indices` attribute to see exactly which keys it created.

```python
gb = df.groupby(['colA','colB'])

# 1) As a dict of group→row‐labels
group_dict = gb.groups
# keys are your group identifiers (tuples if you grouped on multiple columns)
list_of_keys = list(group_dict.keys())
print(list_of_keys)

# 2) Or via the .indices (same keys, but mapping to integer positions)
indices_dict = gb.indices
list_of_keys = list(indices_dict.keys())
print(list_of_keys)
```

Each key in that list is either a single value (if you grouped on one column) or a tuple (if you grouped on multiple columns).
