Exactly—when you call `.transform()`, pandas guarantees the result will have the **same number of rows** and the **same relative ordering** as the original, but it doesn’t reuse the exact same `Index` object. Under the hood it builds a fresh `Series` (often with a new `RangeIndex`) that aligns positionally with your DataFrame. If you ever need the output to share your original index, you can simply reset or explicitly assign `series.index = df.index` after the transform.
