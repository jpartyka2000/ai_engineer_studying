No—Pandas won’t magically group by your MultiIndex just because it’s there. By default, `.groupby()` looks at whatever you pass into its **by** (column labels or arrays) or **level** (index‐level names or positions) parameters. If you don’t tell it to use your index levels, it won’t.

---

### Key Points

1. **No “auto‑grouping”**

   ```python
   df.groupby()  
   # ValueError: You have to supply one of 'by' and 'level'
   ```

   You must supply at least one of:

   * `by=…` – keys in your **columns** (or an array/function that maps each row to a group)
   * `level=…` – names or positions of your **index levels**

2. **Grouping by columns**

   ```python
   # Group by the 'Category' column
   df.groupby('Category')['Revenue'].sum()
   ```

3. **Grouping by index levels**

   ```python
   # Suppose df.index.names == ['Region','Category','Year','Quarter']
   # Group by the 'Year' level of your MultiIndex:
   df.groupby(level='Year')['Revenue'].sum()

   # Or by Region & Category together:
   df.groupby(level=['Region','Category'])['Revenue'].sum()
   ```

4. **Mixing columns & index levels**
   You can even group by a mix of columns and index levels:

   ```python
   df.groupby(
       by=['SomeColumn'],        # a column
       level=['Region','Year']   # two index levels
   )['Revenue'].sum()
   ```

---

### Why `level=` is nicer here

* **No need to `reset_index()`.** You don’t have to pull levels back into columns first.
* **Result keeps your index.** The returned Series/DataFrame will still be indexed by those same level(s).

---

**Bottom line:** if you want to use your MultiIndex in a `groupby`, you must explicitly tell Pandas which level(s) to use via `level=` (or pass index‑level values into `by=`). Otherwise it only considers columns or arrays you hand it.
