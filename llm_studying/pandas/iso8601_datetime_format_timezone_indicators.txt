Excellent question — ISO 8601 defines several **standardized ways to express time zones** within timestamps.

Here’s a complete breakdown 👇

---

## 🕒 ISO 8601 Time Zone Indicators

ISO 8601 timestamps can include **one of three types of timezone information**:

### 1. **UTC “Zulu” indicator**

```
2025-10-20T14:33:05Z
```

* The **`Z`** stands for **Zulu time**, which means **UTC+00:00**.
* It’s the simplest and most common way to mark a timestamp as UTC.

---

### 2. **Numeric UTC offset**

```
2025-10-20T14:33:05+00:00   → UTC (same as Z)
2025-10-20T14:33:05-05:00   → U.S. Central Standard Time (UTC−5)
2025-10-20T14:33:05+09:00   → Japan Standard Time (UTC+9)
2025-10-20T14:33:05+05:30   → India Standard Time (UTC+5:30)
```

* The part after the time (`+hh:mm` or `-hh:mm`) indicates **hours and minutes offset from UTC**.
* A positive offset means “ahead of UTC,” and a negative offset means “behind UTC.”

---

### 3. **No timezone indicator (naive timestamp)**

```
2025-10-20T14:33:05
```

* No `Z` or offset → interpreted as **local time** (no explicit timezone info).
* Depending on your context or parser (like Pandas or Python’s `datetime`), this is treated as **“naive”**, meaning it has **no tzinfo attached**.

---

## 💡 Variants and Notes

| Example                     | Meaning | Notes                                      |
| --------------------------- | ------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| `2025-10-20T14:33:05Z`      | UTC     | Uses “Zulu” suffix                         |
| `2025-10-20T14:33:05+00:00` | UTC     | Equivalent to `Z`                          |
| `2025-10-20T14:33:05-0400`  | UTC−4   | Compact form without colon (allowed)       |
| `2025-10-20T14:33:05+09`    | UTC+9   | Hour-only offset (allowed but less common) |
| `2025-10-20T14:33:05`       | Local   | No timezone indicator                      |

---

## ⚙️ How Pandas Handles This

When you use:

```python
pd.to_datetime(timestamps, format='ISO8601', utc=True)
```

Pandas:

* Automatically detects the timezone part (`Z`, `+05:00`, `-0800`, etc.)
* Converts everything to **UTC**
* Returns timezone-aware datetimes (`datetime64[ns, UTC]`)

Then if you call:

```python
.dt.tz_localize(None)
```

you **drop** the timezone and make them naive (`datetime64[ns]`).
