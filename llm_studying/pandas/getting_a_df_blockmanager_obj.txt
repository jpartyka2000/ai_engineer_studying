Here’s how you can peek at the internal BlockManager of a pandas DataFrame:

```python
# assume df is your DataFrame
bm = df._data
```

* **`df._data`** is the BlockManager object that pandas uses under the hood to store all your column blocks.

Once you have it, you can inspect its blocks:

```python
for blk in bm.blocks:
    print(f"Block dtype={blk.dtype}, shape={blk.shape}, columns={blk.mgr_locs.as_array()}")
```

* **`blk.dtype`** tells you the NumPy dtype of that block.
* **`blk.shape`** is `(n_rows, n_columns_in_block)`.
* **`blk.mgr_locs`** gives you the positions of those columns in your DataFrame.

---

> **Caution:**
> • `._data` is a private attribute and may change in future pandas releases.
> • For most use-cases you don’t need to touch it—this is purely for deep introspection or debugging.
