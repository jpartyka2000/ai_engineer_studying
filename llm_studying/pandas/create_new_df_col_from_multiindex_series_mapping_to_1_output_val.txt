You can turn your MultiIndex Series into a lookup that you apply to every row in your DataFrame by first building a “tuple‐key” and then using `.map()`. Here’s the pattern:

```python
# 1) Assume you already have a Series like this:
#    idx = MultiIndex(levels=[…], codes=[…], names=['DEGREE_OFFERING','YEAR_QUARTER','COHORT_TS'])
#    series_cohort_rates = pd.Series(data=…, index=idx)

# 2) In your df, build a column of key‐tuples:
df['lookup_key'] = list(zip(
    df['DEGREE_OFFERING'],
    df['YEAR_QUARTER'],
    df['COHORT_TS']
))

# 3) Map those tuples through your Series:
df['seasonal_admit_rate'] = df['lookup_key'].map(series_cohort_rates)

# 4) (Optional) clean up
df.drop(columns='lookup_key', inplace=True)
```

### Why it works

* `series_cohort_rates` has an index of 3 levels:

  ```
  ('usc-dpt','2022-Q3',Timestamp('2024-06-01')) → 0.05985
  ('usc-dpt','2022-Q3',Timestamp('2023-06-01')) → 0.04387
   …
  ```
* By zipping your three columns into a tuple, you get exactly the same keys that live in `series_cohort_rates.index`.
* `.map(...)` then looks up each tuple in the Series and returns its value (or `NaN` if the tuple isn’t present).

This is fully vectorized (no row-wise `apply`) and will give you a new column aligned one-to-one with your original DataFrame rows.
