Yes, LightGBM inherently creates an ensemble of gradient boosted decision trees (GBDTs) — that's exactly how it works.

Here’s what that means:

In LightGBM, the model itself is an ensemble (i.e., a collection) of decision trees.

These trees are trained sequentially, where each new tree corrects the errors made by the previous ones.

This is different from a random forest, where trees are trained independently in parallel (using bagging, not boosting).


So to clarify:

Yes — LightGBM builds a forest of trees, but they are boosted trees trained sequentially (not bagged like in Random Forest).

If you train a LightGBM model with, say, n_estimators=100, then you're training an ensemble of 100 GBDTs, where each tree tries to improve upon the predictions of the previous ones.
