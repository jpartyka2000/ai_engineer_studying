A **leaf score** (sometimes called a **leaf weight**) is simply the constant value that a single leaf of a boosted tree predicts.  In gradient boosting we choose that constant so as to minimize the regularized objective over the examples that fall into that leaf.

---

### 1. The basic formula (with only L2 regularization)

For a given leaf $l$, let

* $G = \sum_{i\in\text{leaf}} g_i$ be the sum of the first‑order gradients (w\.r.t. the loss) of all training examples in that leaf,
* $H = \sum_{i\in\text{leaf}} h_i$ be the sum of the second‑order gradients (Hessians),
* $\lambda$ be the L2 regularization parameter (`reg_lambda`).

Then the optimal leaf score $w^\star$ is

$$
w^\star
\;=\;
-\frac{G}{H + \lambda}.
$$

---

### 2. A tiny numeric example

Suppose in one leaf you have three training examples whose gradients and Hessians (from your loss function) are:

| example | $g_i$ | $h_i$ |
| :-----: | :---: | :---: |
|    1    |  $-2$ |  1.5  |
|    2    |  $-3$ |  2.0  |
|    3    |  $+1$ |  1.0  |

and you’ve set `reg_lambda = 1.0`.  Then:

1. **Sum the gradients**
   $G = -2 + (-3) + 1 = -4$

2. **Sum the Hessians**
   $H = 1.5 + 2.0 + 1.0 = 4.5$

3. **Compute the leaf score**

   $$
     w^\star
     = -\frac{-4}{4.5 + 1.0}
     = \frac{4}{5.5}
     \approx 0.727.
   $$

That means *any* new sample that falls into this leaf will have its prediction adjusted by about **+0.727** by this tree.

---

### 3. Effect of L1 regularization

If you also have `reg_alpha > 0`, the formula becomes a *soft‑thresholding*:

$$
w^\star
= 
\begin{cases}
\displaystyle
\frac{\,-(\,|G| - \alpha)\,\mathrm{sign}(G)\,}{\,H + \lambda\,}
& \text{if } |G| > \alpha,\\[8pt]
0
& \text{otherwise.}
\end{cases}
$$

So small‑magnitude gradient sums get zeroed out, pruning tiny leaves entirely.

---

### 4. Putting it in context

* Each tree in LightGBM is a collection of these leaves.
* During training, for every candidate split it computes the would‑be $G_L, H_L$ and $G_R, H_R$ on the two sides, and uses that to score the split (the gain in loss reduction).
* Once the tree structure is fixed, it computes one $w^\star$ per leaf using the formula above.

That’s what your “leaf score” is: the per‑leaf constant that best fits the residual gradients, regularized by `reg_lambda` (and optionally `reg_alpha`).
