The `verbose_eval` parameter controls **how often** (and whether) LightGBM prints out evaluation metrics during training (or CV). It works in both `lgb.train()` and `lgb.cv()`. Here’s how it behaves:

- **`verbose_eval=True`**  
  Print the evaluation results **every** iteration (i.e. every tree/round).

- **`verbose_eval=False`** (or `0`)  
  Suppress all per‑iteration logging.

- **`verbose_eval=k`** (an integer > 0)  
  Print the evaluation results **every** _k_ iterations.  
  ```python
  # print metrics every 50 rounds
  model = lgb.train(
      params,
      train_data,
      num_boost_round=500,
      valid_sets=[train_data, valid_data],
      verbose_eval=50,          # logs at rounds 50, 100, 150, …
      early_stopping_rounds=20
  )
  ```

- **In CV**  
  ```python
  cv_results = lgb.cv(
      params,
      dtrain,
      num_boost_round=200,
      nfold=5,
      verbose_eval=25,          # show aggregated fold metrics every 25 rounds
      early_stopping_rounds=10
  )
  ```

Use `verbose_eval` to get just enough feedback on training progress without flooding your console.
