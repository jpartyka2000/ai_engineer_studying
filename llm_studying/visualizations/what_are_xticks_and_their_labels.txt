Not exactly—an **xtick** is just a marker (and optional label) at a given x-coordinate on the axis. In a bar plot:

- **Each bar** occupies a “category” position (you might think of that as a “bin” when you’re grouping data).  
- **Each xtick** is usually placed at the center of a bar (or group of bars) and shows the category label.

So:

- If you have 4 bars (or 4 groups of bars), you’ll typically have 4 xticks—one per bar-center.  
- But the **tick** itself is just the little line (and text) on the axis. The **bar/bin** is the graphical rectangle whose height represents your value.

---

### Example

```python
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

# Suppose we have 4 categories:
categories = ['A','B','C','D']
values     = [0.1,0.3,0.5,0.7]

fig, ax = plt.subplots()
bars = ax.bar(range(len(categories)), values)

# By default, ticks at x = [0,1,2,3] with labels ['A','B','C','D']
ax.set_xticks(range(len(categories)))      # xticks at 0,1,2,3
ax.set_xticklabels(categories)             # labels at those ticks
plt.show()
```

- Here the **bins** are the bars at x=0,1,2,3.  
- The **xticks** are also at x=0,1,2,3—but you could put ticks anywhere (even between bars) if you wanted.

---

### In Your Grouped-Bar Context

When you do:

```python
ax.set_xticks([0,1,2,3])
ax.set_xticklabels(['0.0–0.2','0.2–0.5','0.5–0.8','0.8–1.0'])
```

you’re telling matplotlib:

1. “Put ticks (those little lines) at x-positions 0,1,2,3.”  
2. “Label them with the bin-range strings.”

The bars themselves will be drawn centered on those same x-coordinates (that’s why the ticks line up with the bins), but the concepts remain distinct:

- **Bar/bin** = the data rectangle.  
- **Tick** = the axis marker/label.

Hope that clears it up!
