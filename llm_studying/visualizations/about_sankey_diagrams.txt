A Sankey diagram shows **flows between categories** using arrows or bands whose **width represents quantity**.

The classic example is an energy flow diagram: energy enters from sources on the left (coal, solar, gas), flows through transformations in the middle (power plants), and exits as end uses on the right (heating, transport, waste). The thickness of each band shows how much energy flows along that path.

Key characteristics:
- Nodes are categories or stages
- Links between nodes show flow/transition
- Link width encodes magnitude
- Often arranged left-to-right to show progression through stages

For your use case, you might have stages like: Seed Question → Generation 1 → Generation 2 → etc., with bands showing how many questions flow from each category/strategy to the next. It would reveal patterns like "40% of seed questions become performance_analysis, but by generation 3 most have shifted to other categories."
