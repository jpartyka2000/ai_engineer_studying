The user wants to "move the line up" in their seaborn countplot because the annotation overlaps the top border. A good solution is to adjust the y-axis limit after plotting. Setting `ax.set_ylim(0, counts.max() * 1.1)` extends the y-limit to include space above the bars and annotations. Thought for 13 seconds

You just need to give yourself a little breathing room above the tallest bar by expanding the y‑axis limits before drawing your annotations. For example, right after you draw the countplot you can do:

```python
# after sns.countplot...
ax = sns.countplot(x=col, data=df, order=order)

# bump the top of the y‑axis out by 10%
max_count = counts.max()
ax.set_ylim(0, max_count * 1.1)

# now annotate
for patch in ax.patches:
    height = patch.get_height()
    ax.text(
        patch.get_x() + patch.get_width() / 2,
        height + max_count * 0.01,
        int(height),
        ha='center', va='bottom'
    )
```

Or even more succinctly:

```python
ax.margins(y=0.1)
```

which automatically adds 10% padding above (and below) your tallest bar so that your labels never overlap the top spine.
