Wrap the fraction in parentheses and then apply the exponent:

```latex
\left( \frac{a}{b} \right)^n
```

This renders as the fraction (a/b) raised to the power n, with the parentheses sized to match the fraction height.

If you want it without the parentheses visually (just the exponent floating to the upper right of the fraction), you can do:

```latex
{\frac{a}{b}}^n
```

But this often looks awkward and is harder to read. The parenthesized version is standard mathematical notation.
