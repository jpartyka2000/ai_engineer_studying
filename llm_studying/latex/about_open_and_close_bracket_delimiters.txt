In LaTeX, `\[` and `\]` are delimiters for **displayed math mode** (also called display math or display style).

When you write:
```latex
\[
  E = mc^2
\]
```

This creates a mathematical equation that is:
- **Centered on its own line** (displayed, not inline with text)
- **Rendered in display style**, which means larger symbols, bigger fractions, etc.

This is equivalent to the older `$$...$$` syntax, though `\[...\]` is now preferred in LaTeX.

**Comparison:**

- **Inline math**: `$E = mc^2$` or `\(E = mc^2\)` — renders within the text line
- **Display math**: `\[E = mc^2\]` or `$$E = mc^2$$` — renders centered on its own line

So `\[` opens display math mode and `\]` closes it.
