Great observation — that `"owned_by"` field is a little opaque if you haven’t dug into it before. Here’s the breakdown:

* **`"owned_by": "openai"`**
  These are the **base models** (like `gpt-4o`, `gpt-4.1`, `gpt-3.5-turbo`, etc.). They’re trained and hosted by OpenAI and available across orgs/projects. You don’t “own” them directly — you just reference them.

* **`"owned_by": "system"`**
  This typically shows up for **fine-tuned models** or **special project-scoped variants**. “system” here doesn’t mean OpenAI-wide; it means the model is tied to the *system project* (i.e., the internal account/project context under which the fine-tune was created).
  In practice, these are models created within your org/project but managed under OpenAI’s hosting infrastructure. They’re “system-owned” because OpenAI’s backend is the actual custodian, but **you (your project) have access rights**.

* **Other values (like `org_xxx` or project IDs)**
  In some SDK versions or API responses, you may instead see the owning **org** or **project** string explicitly. That’s the clearest indicator of where the model lives.

---

✅ **Rule of thumb**:

* `"openai"` = base model
* `"system"` = a model instance that exists only because of your fine-tune or your project context
* `"org_xxx"` / `"proj_xxx"` = explicit ownership by your org/project
