Good question — these two terms are related but not the same thing. Here’s how they differ:

---

## **1. Learning Rate (LR)**

* The **base step size** used by your optimizer to update model parameters.
* Controls how big a jump you make in the direction of the gradient each step.
* Example: in SGD,

  $$
  \theta_{t+1} = \theta_t - \eta \cdot \nabla L(\theta_t)
  $$

  where $\eta$ = learning rate.
* Typical values: $10^{-5}$ to $10^{-1}$, depending on model and optimizer.

---

## **2. Learning Rate Multiplier (LR multiplier)**

* A **scaling factor** applied on top of the base learning rate.
* Used to adjust the learning rate for:

  * Specific layers (e.g., train head faster than backbone in transfer learning).
  * Certain parameter groups (e.g., biases vs. weights).
  * Schedulers or fine-tuning strategies.
* Example:

  ```python
  base_lr = 1e-4
  param_group = {"params": model.head.parameters(), "lr": base_lr * 10}
  ```

  Here, the **multiplier is 10** for the head, so its effective learning rate is `1e-3`.

---

## **3. Why Use Multipliers?**

* **Transfer learning:** Freeze backbone or give it a small multiplier (e.g., 0.1×) while training the new head with a larger multiplier (e.g., 10×).
* **Fine-tuning LLMs:** Sometimes only adapter layers (LoRA, prefixes) get a higher multiplier to adapt quickly, while the main model stays slow/stable.
* **Custom schedulers:** Some frameworks let you define a base LR schedule and apply per-layer multipliers without writing separate schedules.

---

## **4. Summary**

| Term                         | Meaning                                                                                    | Example                                  |
| ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------- |
| **Learning rate**            | The actual step size used by the optimizer for updates.                                    | `1e-4`                                   |
| **Learning rate multiplier** | A factor applied to the base learning rate to modify it for specific parameters or groups. | multiplier = 0.1 → effective LR = `1e-5` |

---

✅ **In short:**

* The **learning rate** is the raw step size in training.
* A **learning rate multiplier** scales that base rate for specific parameters, layers, or phases of training.
