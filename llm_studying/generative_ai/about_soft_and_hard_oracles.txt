In the context of SFT (Supervised Fine-Tuning) of LLMs, **soft oracles** refer to models or systems that provide probabilistic or "soft" labels/feedback rather than hard, binary correct/incorrect labels.

## Key Characteristics of Soft Oracles

1. **Probability Distributions**: Instead of saying "this answer is correct" (hard label), a soft oracle provides probability distributions over possible answers, like "70% correct, 30% could be better"

2. **Confidence Scores**: They output confidence levels for their predictions, acknowledging uncertainty

3. **Gradient Information**: Soft oracles provide richer gradient signals during training compared to hard labels

## Common Types in LLM Fine-Tuning

### 1. **Teacher Models in Distillation**
```python
# Example: Using a larger model as a soft oracle
teacher_logits = large_model(input)  # Returns probability distribution
student_loss = KL_divergence(student_logits, teacher_logits)
```

### 2. **Reward Models with Uncertainty**
Instead of binary feedback:
- Hard oracle: "Response A is better than B" (1 or 0)
- Soft oracle: "Response A is 0.73 better than B" (continuous value)

### 3. **Ensemble-based Oracles**
Multiple models vote, providing a distribution of opinions rather than a single answer.

## Why Soft Oracles Matter in SFT

1. **More Information**: A soft label like [0.7, 0.2, 0.1] contains more information than a hard label like [1, 0, 0]

2. **Better Generalization**: Training on probability distributions often leads to models that generalize better

3. **Handling Ambiguity**: Many tasks have inherently ambiguous "correct" answers - soft oracles can represent this uncertainty

## Practical Example in Your Context

In your fine-tuning script, if you were using a soft oracle approach, instead of training on exact responses like:
```json
{"messages": [{"role": "assistant", "content": "Exact answer"}]}
```

You might have:
```json
{
  "messages": [{"role": "assistant", "content": "Answer"}],
  "confidence": 0.85,
  "alternative_responses": [
    {"content": "Alternative 1", "score": 0.10},
    {"content": "Alternative 2", "score": 0.05}
  ]
}
```

## Contrast with Hard Oracles

- **Hard Oracle**: "This is THE correct answer" (binary supervision)
- **Soft Oracle**: "This answer has 0.8 probability of being appropriate" (probabilistic supervision)

Soft oracles are particularly useful when fine-tuning on subjective tasks where there isn't always one definitively correct answer, like creative writing, dialogue generation, or complex reasoning tasks.
