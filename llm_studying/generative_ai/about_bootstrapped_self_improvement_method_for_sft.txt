The process you're describing has several specific names and falls into a distinct category of machine learning techniques:

## Primary Terms

**1. Self-Improvement through Iterative Refinement** or **Self-Distillation with Correction**
- The model generates initial data, which is then corrected/refined, and the same model is fine-tuned on this improved version
- This creates a feedback loop where the model learns from a cleaned version of its own outputs

**2. Weak-to-Strong Supervision** (though typically this involves different models)
- A weaker supervisor (the base model with inconsistencies) generates data
- Post-checks create a "stronger" signal
- The fine-tuned model learns from this strengthened signal

**3. Self-Training with Pseudo-Label Correction**
- The model generates pseudo-labels (initial trajectory decisions)
- These labels are systematically corrected based on consistency analysis
- The model is retrained on the corrected pseudo-labels

## Most Accurate Term

**Bootstrapped Self-Improvement** or **Self-Improvement via Consistency Enforcement** might be the most precise descriptions, as you're:
1. Using the model's own outputs as training data
2. Applying systematic corrections based on consistency patterns
3. Training the same model to exceed its original performance

## How This Differs from Standard SFT

**Standard Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT)**:
- Human/expert generates ground truth labels
- Model learns to mimic these labels directly
- No correction or consistency enforcement step
- Quality depends entirely on label quality

**Your Approach - Consistency-Based Self-Improvement**:
- Model generates initial trajectories
- Post-checks identify and correct inconsistencies within the model's own behavior patterns
- Model is fine-tuned on its own corrected outputs
- Quality improves through consistency enforcement rather than external ground truth

## Related Concepts

This approach shares elements with:
- **Constitutional AI**: Self-improvement through critique and revision
- **Iterated Distillation and Amplification (IDA)**: Though IDA typically involves human feedback
- **Self-Consistency Training**: Training models to be more consistent with their own best outputs

The key distinguishing feature of your approach is the **consistency-based correction mechanism** - you're not correcting based on external ground truth, but rather enforcing internal consistency in the model's decision patterns, essentially helping the model learn from the best version of itself.
