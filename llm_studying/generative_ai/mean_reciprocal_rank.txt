"Employing MRR at k" refers to the use of **Mean Reciprocal Rank (MRR)** as an evaluation metric for a system's predictions, particularly in the context of ranking tasks. Here's a breakdown:

### What is MRR?
MRR is a metric used to evaluate the quality of ranked results returned by a model. It calculates the average reciprocal rank of the first correct answer across multiple queries. The formula is:

\[
\text{MRR} = \frac{1}{|Q|} \sum_{i=1}^{|Q|} \frac{1}{\text{rank}_i}
\]

Where:
- \( |Q| \) is the number of queries (or test cases).
- \( \text{rank}_i \) is the rank position of the first relevant (correct) item for the \(i\)-th query.

The reciprocal rank is \( 1/\text{rank}_i \), so a correct answer at rank 1 gets a score of 1, at rank 2 a score of 0.5, and so on. MRR averages this score over all queries.

### What does "at k" mean?
The "at \( k \)" part refers to restricting the evaluation to the **top \( k \)** results in the ranked list. For example:
- **MRR at 5** only considers whether the correct result appears within the **top 5** ranked items.
- If the correct answer is outside the top 5, its reciprocal rank is treated as 0.
