No, FLUX.2 [max] can't do this. FLUX models are **image generation** models (text-to-image), not evaluation/scoring models. They take a text prompt and produce an image — they don't take an image+prompt pair and return an adherence score.

What you're looking for is typically called **text-image alignment scoring**. Here are some approaches that can do this:

**CLIPScore** — Uses OpenAI's CLIP model to compute cosine similarity between a text embedding and an image embedding. Returns a score you can normalize to 0–1. This is the most common and straightforward approach.

**ImageReward** — A model specifically trained on human preference data to score how well generated images match prompts. Gives more nuanced, human-aligned scores than CLIP.

**BLIPv2 / LLaVA / GPT-4o** — You can use a vision-language model to evaluate the image against the prompt and ask it to return a numerical score. Less standardized but can capture semantic nuances that CLIP misses.

**HPSv2 (Human Preference Score)** — Another learned metric trained on human preference data for text-to-image alignment.

For most use cases, **CLIPScore** is the easiest starting point, and **ImageReward** is the best if you want scores that correlate well with human judgment.
