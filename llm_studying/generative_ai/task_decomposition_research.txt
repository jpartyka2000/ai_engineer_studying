Yes, there is ongoing research evaluating Large Language Models (LLMs) on their ability to decompose complex questions into a series of tasks. This process, known as task decomposition, is crucial for enhancing the interpretability and performance of LLMs on multifaceted problems.

Key Research and Evaluation Frameworks:

Decomposed Prompting (DecomP):

Approach: DecomP involves breaking down complex tasks into simpler sub-tasks, each handled by an LLM or a specialized handler. This modular approach aims to improve the model's performance on intricate tasks by addressing each component individually.
Evaluation: Researchers assess the effectiveness of DecomP by comparing the performance of LLMs on decomposed tasks versus holistic task handling, analyzing improvements in accuracy and efficiency.
HD-Eval (Hierarchical Criteria Decomposition):

Approach: HD-Eval proposes an iterative framework that aligns LLM-based evaluators with human preferences through hierarchical decomposition of evaluation criteria. This method breaks down evaluation tasks into finer-grained components to enhance alignment and coverage.

Decompose and Aggregate Framework:

Approach: Inspired by pedagogical evaluation rubrics, this framework decomposes the evaluation process into distinct stages, allowing for a step-by-step assessment of LLMs' capabilities.
Evaluation: By analyzing each stage separately, researchers gain interpretable insights into the LLM's performance on complex tasks, leading to targeted improvements. citeturn0academia11
ADAPT (As-Needed Decomposition and Planning):

Approach: ADAPT addresses limitations in LLM-based methods for complex decision-making by employing recursive decomposition and planning. It adapts to task complexity and the LLM's capabilities, facilitating dynamic task handling.
Evaluation: The approach is evaluated based on the LLM's ability to autonomously decompose tasks and plan solutions, enhancing performance on multi-step queries. citeturn0search4
