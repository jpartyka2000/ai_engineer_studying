That's a big question with a wide range depending on what you mean by "music generation LLM." Let me break it down by the main approaches and scale.

**Training compute is the dominant cost.** The key variables are model size, dataset size, and training duration.

**Small-scale / research model** (think Riffusion, MusicGen-small level):
- ~300M-1.5B parameters
- Training on ~10K-100K hours of audio
- 32-128 A100 GPUs for 1-4 weeks
- **$50K-$500K** in compute alone

**Mid-scale** (MusicGen-large, Stable Audio level):
- ~3B-7B parameters
- 100K+ hours of licensed/curated audio
- 256-512 A100/H100 GPUs for 2-8 weeks
- **$500K-$5M** in compute

**Frontier-scale** (competing with Suno, Udio, Google's MusicFX):
- 7B-30B+ parameters, likely multimodal architecture
- Massive curated dataset (millions of tracks)
- Thousands of GPUs for months
- **$10M-$100M+** in compute

Then add on top of that:

- **Data licensing/acquisition**: This is the hidden killer. Licensed music datasets are expensive or don't exist at scale. Suno and Udio are both facing major copyright lawsuits. Using only public domain, Creative Commons, or self-generated synthetic data severely limits quality. Budget anywhere from $0 (if you only use open datasets like MusicCaps, FMA, MTG-Jamendo) to millions for licensed catalogs.

- **Team**: Even a lean team of 3-5 ML researchers/engineers for a year runs $1-3M in salary. Frontier efforts have teams of 20-50+.

- **Infrastructure beyond training**: Data pipelines, evaluation, hosting, iteration. Roughly 30-50% on top of raw compute.

**Realistic estimates for an open-source project:**

| Ambition | Total Cost | Comparable To |
|---|---|---|
| Research/proof-of-concept | $100K-$500K | MusicGen-small |
| Competitive open-source | $2M-$10M | Stable Audio Open |
| Frontier competitive | $50M-$200M+ | Suno/Udio quality |

The most capital-efficient path would be fine-tuning an existing open-source audio model (like Stable Audio Open or MusicGen) rather than training from scratch — that could get you interesting results for $10K-$50K in compute. Is that closer to what you're considering, or are you thinking about training something from scratch?
