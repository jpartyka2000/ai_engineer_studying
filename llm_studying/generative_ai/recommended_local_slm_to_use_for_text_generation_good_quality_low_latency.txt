For music reviews and genre descriptions, I'd recommend **Mistral 7B** or **Llama 2 7B** as your best options. Here's why they work well for your needs:

**Mistral 7B** is probably your top choice. It's well-regarded for coherent, natural-sounding text generation, handles creative writing reasonably well, and has strong performance-to-size ratio. The base model is around 14GB, but quantized versions (4-bit or 8-bit) can shrink this to 4-5GB while maintaining good quality.

**Llama 2 7B** is also solid and widely available. It's proven to work well for various writing tasks including creative content. Similar file size story—the base is larger but quantized versions are very manageable.

For even smaller footprints, consider **Phi 2** or **Gemma 7B**, though these may require more careful prompting to match the quality of Mistral or Llama.

**A few practical tips:**

Use a tool like **Ollama** or **LM Studio** to run these locally—they handle model management and quantization for you, making it dead simple. Just download and run.

For file size, quantize to **4-bit or 8-bit**. You'll lose minimal quality for text generation while cutting file sizes dramatically. A 4-bit quantized 7B model typically runs 3-5GB.

For latency, a 7B model on a decent GPU (even mid-range like RTX 3060) will give you response times of 1-2 seconds. CPU-only will be slower but still reasonable.

Since you're specifically writing reviews, you might get good results by giving the model a few examples of the style you want (in your prompt/system message), which can work well with these smaller models.
