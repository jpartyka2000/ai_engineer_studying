## Step Calculation Formula
**Steps = (Number of Examples × Number of Epochs) ÷ Batch Size**

For your job:
- **Examples**: 10
- **Epochs**: 5  
- **Batch Size**: 2

**Steps = (10 × 5) ÷ 2 = 50 ÷ 2 = 25 steps**

## What Each Step Represents
- Each step processes one **batch** of data
- With batch size = 2, each step processes 2 examples
- Over 5 epochs, your 10 examples are seen 50 times total
- Those 50 example presentations are grouped into 25 batches/steps

## Epoch Breakdown
- **Epoch 1**: Examples 1-10 → Steps 1-5 (5 steps, 2 examples per step)
- **Epoch 2**: Examples 1-10 → Steps 6-10
- **Epoch 3**: Examples 1-10 → Steps 11-15
- **Epoch 4**: Examples 1-10 → Steps 16-20
- **Epoch 5**: Examples 1-10 → Steps 21-25

## Why This Matters
With only 10 examples and 5 epochs, your model sees each example exactly 5 times during training. This is actually quite limited - most fine-tuning jobs benefit from:
- More training examples (hundreds to thousands)
- Or more epochs if you have limited data
- Careful monitoring to avoid overfitting with small datasets

Your setup will work, but consider whether 25 total training steps provide enough learning opportunities for your specific use case.
