Here’s an overview of OpenAI’s **open-weight reasoning models** (“gpt-oss”) as presented on the OpenAI Open Models page ([OpenAI][1]):

---

## What Open Models Are

* **Open-weight**: Released under the **Apache 2.0 license**, so you can run, fine-tune, and deploy them commercially without copyleft or patent concerns. ([OpenAI][1])
* **Agentic**: Built to follow instructions deeply, emit full chain-of-thought, and orchestrate tools (web search, Python execution) within the same run. ([OpenAI][1])
* **Customizable**: You can adjust “reasoning effort” (low/medium/high) and apply full-parameter fine-tuning to adapt them to your specific tasks. ([OpenAI][1])

---

## Available Models

| Model            | Size         | Deployment Targets                      |
| ---------------- | ------------ | --------------------------------------- |
| **gpt-oss-120b** | 120 B params | Data centers, high-end desktops/laptops |
| **gpt-oss-20b**  | 20 B params  | Most desktops and laptops               |

> Both models are downloadable on Hugging Face or GitHub and runnable locally or in your cloud environment. ([OpenAI][1])

---

## Performance Snapshot

On benchmarks comparing to OpenAI’s internally hosted models:

| Benchmark            | gpt-oss-120b | gpt-oss-20b | OpenAI o3 | OpenAI o4-mini |
| -------------------- | ------------ | ----------- | --------- | -------------- |
| **MMLU**             | 90.0         | 85.3        | 93.4      | 93.0           |
| **GPQA Diamond**     | 80.1         | 71.5        | 83.3      | 81.4           |
| **AIME 2024 (math)** | 96.6         | 96.0        | 95.2      | 98.7           |
| **AIME 2025 (math)** | 97.9         | 98.7        | 98.4      | 99.5           |

This shows that gpt-oss-120b closes much of the gap to proprietary  models on knowledge and reasoning benchmarks ([OpenAI][1]).

## Built-in Safety & Evaluation

* **Rigorous safety training** and evaluation under OpenAI’s Preparedness Framework, including testing against malicious fine-tunes. ([OpenAI][1])
* **System cards** published for transparency on model capabilities and limitations.

---

## Getting Started

1. **Download** the model weights from Hugging Face or GitHub.
2. **Load** via Hugging Face Transformers or other runtimes (Ollama, vLLM).
3. **Fine-tune** or adjust chain-of-thought settings to match your use case.

You can also try them live in the **gpt-oss playground** on the OpenAI site. ([OpenAI][1])
