Yes, generally. Text rendering quality correlates strongly with overall model quality, though it's not a perfectly linear relationship — some models are specifically optimized for text while others treat it as a secondary concern.

The reason is that rendering legible text in an image is one of the hardest things for diffusion models to do. The model has to understand letter shapes, spelling, spatial arrangement, and how text interacts with the scene — all at the pixel level. Cheaper/faster models that take fewer denoising steps or use smaller architectures tend to produce more text errors because they don't have enough capacity or refinement passes to get individual letterforms right.

In practice, the hierarchy roughly looks like this for text accuracy:

**Best text rendering:** FLUX 1.1 Pro / FLUX.2 Pro, Ideogram 3.0, Imagen 4 — these can reliably spell multi-word phrases, render them in context-appropriate styles, and handle things like signs, posters, and UI mockups.

**Good but imperfect:** FLUX.1 dev, Midjourney v7, DALL-E 3 — usually get short words right, but start making mistakes on longer phrases, repeated letters, or uncommon words.

**Weak text rendering:** FLUX.1 schnell, Stable Diffusion XL, older Midjourney versions — frequently garble words, swap letters, add extra characters, or produce gibberish that looks like text from a distance but falls apart up close.

The relationship between model quality and text accuracy comes from the same underlying capability — the model's ability to follow complex prompts precisely and maintain fine-grained spatial coherence. A model that renders skin pores and fabric weave accurately is using the same spatial precision that allows it to get the difference between "E" and "F" right.

That said, the **steps parameter** matters here too. Even a high-quality model will produce worse text at 4 steps than at 28 steps, because text details are among the last things to converge during the denoising process. So model quality sets the ceiling, but steps determine how close you get to it.
