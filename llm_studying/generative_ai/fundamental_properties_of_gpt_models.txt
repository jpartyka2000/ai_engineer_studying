GPT (Generative Pre-trained Transformer) models share several defining characteristics:

## Core Architecture

**Transformer-based**: GPTs use the transformer architecture, specifically the decoder-only variant. This relies on self-attention mechanisms that allow the model to weigh the relevance of different parts of the input when generating each token.

**Autoregressive generation**: GPTs generate text one token at a time, left-to-right, with each new token conditioned on all previous tokens. This means they predict "what comes next" based on everything before.

## Training Approach

**Pre-training on massive text corpora**: GPTs are trained on enormous amounts of text data (books, websites, code, etc.) using a simple objective: predict the next token. This unsupervised learning allows the model to absorb patterns in language, facts, reasoning styles, and more.

**Transfer learning**: The "pre-trained" part means the model learns general language understanding first, then can be fine-tuned or prompted for specific tasks without task-specific architecture changes.

## Key Properties

**In-context learning**: GPTs can adapt to new tasks from examples provided in the prompt, without updating their weights. You can show them a few examples and they'll generalize.

**Emergent capabilities**: As GPTs scale (more parameters, more data, more compute), they develop capabilities not explicitly trained for—like arithmetic, translation, or coding—that emerge from the pre-training objective.

**No explicit knowledge retrieval**: Unlike retrieval systems, GPTs store knowledge implicitly in their parameters, which means they can be confidently wrong and have a knowledge cutoff.

**Prompt sensitivity**: Output quality depends heavily on how you phrase the input.
