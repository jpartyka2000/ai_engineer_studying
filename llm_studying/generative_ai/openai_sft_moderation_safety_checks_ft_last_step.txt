## Safety Validation Step

OpenAI performs automatic safety validation on fine-tuning jobs to ensure compliance with their usage policies. To preserve the default model's safety features through the fine-tuning process, fine-tuning training data is passed through our Moderation API and a GPT‑4 powered moderation system to detect unsafe training data that conflict with our safety standards.

## What Gets Checked

The safety validation process examines your training data for:

- **Harmful content types**: Including hate speech, violence, self-harm, and discriminatory content
- **Policy violations**: Content that conflicts with OpenAI's usage policies
- **Safety standards**: Ensuring the fine-tuned model maintains appropriate safety guardrails

## The Process

1. **Data Screening**: Your training data is automatically evaluated during the fine-tuning job
2. **Dual Moderation**: The system uses both the Moderation API and a GPT-4 powered moderation system
3. **Policy Enforcement**: If violations are detected, the fine-tuning job may be rejected or flagged

## Common Issues

Even after heavy filtration and moderation of the dataset, my fine-tuned model still occasionally generates responses that violate the usage policy (discriminatory/violent language). This shows that the validation process is quite thorough and can catch issues even in pre-filtered datasets.

## Best Practices

- Pre-filter your training data through the Moderation API before submitting
- Review OpenAI's usage policies carefully
- Remove any potentially problematic content from your training examples
- Consider the context and potential misuse of your fine-tuned model
