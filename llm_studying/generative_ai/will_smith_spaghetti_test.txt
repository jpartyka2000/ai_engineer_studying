What Is the Will Smith Spaghetti Test?

It originated from a viral, AI-generated video first shared in March 2023 by a Reddit user. The clip portrayed a distorted, surreal depiction of Will Smith eating spaghetti—his features morphing unnaturally, chewing motions bizarrely fluid, creating a uncanny and unsettling effect  .

Because of its oddness and memorability, the clip became a meme and evolved into an unofficial benchmark: can an AI faithfully depict Will Smith realistically eating spaghetti—facial expressions, human mannerisms, food texture, everything included?  .



---

Why It Matters

The test is valued for its simplicity and memorability. Unlike technical benchmarks (e.g. ImageNet classification accuracy, language model reasoning), the Will Smith clip instantly shows whether an AI video generator can produce believable human behavior in motion  .

As example: In May 2025, Google's Veo 3, unveiled at I/O 2025, generated a version of the video that was surprisingly photorealistic—lighting, textures, facial nuance, and motion were much more lifelike. Some even said it "passed" the spaghetti test in terms of visual fidelity  .

However, despite the improvements, minor oddities—like “crunchy” sound effects or slight inconsistencies—still give away the AI nature  .



---

Community Perspectives

On Reddit, this test has drawn both humor and criticism:

> “The way the benchmark is real life mimicry, then there isn't exactly much room left to reach.”
“It has been a bad benchmark… Now you can just overfit to that video, and get a good result even though your model sucks at anything else.” 



These comments underscore the concern that models might overfit—fine-tuning to “Will Smith eating spaghetti” without broader generative skill improvements.

In Summary

The Will Smith spaghetti test is a popular, informal litmus test in AI video generation communities.

It began with a bizarre, glitchy viral clip and has since become a touchstone for progress in rendering lifelike human behavior.

Major models like Google's Veo 3 have made dramatic strides toward realism—but the test remains more symbolic than conclusive.

Ultimately, while fun and illustrative, it's just one quirky indicator among many of AI’s evolving video capabilities.
