There isn’t a dedicated “get fine‐tune job cost” endpoint in the OpenAI API—so you can’t just call something like `client.fine_tunes.get_cost(job_id)`. Fine-tune jobs themselves don’t return a “cost” field.

**How to figure out what you spent:**

1. **Usage Records**
   Use the [Usage API endpoint](https://platform.openai.com/docs/api-reference/usage) to pull your token usage broken down by `model` and `purpose`. Fine-tuning shows up under the `fine_tune` category. By querying the date range that covers your job’s run time and summing the `usage` × `unit_price` for that category, you can approximate the cost.

2. **Billing Dashboard / Invoices**
   The simplest way is to look in the OpenAI web dashboard under **Billing → Usage** or inspect your monthly invoice PDF, which will show line items for fine-tuning.

3. **Track Ahead of Time**
   If you need per-job granularity in your own system, you can instrument your pipeline to:

   * Record the job’s `created_at` and `updated_at` timestamps.
   * Immediately after it finishes, call the Usage API for that exact time window.
   * Multiply token counts by the published fine-tuning rates.

While a bit manual, that approach is the only way to programmatically attribute specific fine-tune jobs to dollars spent.
