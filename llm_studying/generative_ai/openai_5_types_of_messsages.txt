In the OpenAI Chat—and especially the Harmony framing for gpt-oss—every message carries one of **five roles**, each indicating its origin or purpose:

1. **`system`**

   * **Who it is:** The platform or model itself.
   * **What it does:** Conveys global context or instructions that sit above everything else—things like the model’s identity, knowledge cutoff date, or broad behavioral directives (e.g. “You are a helpful assistant.”).
   * **Visibility:** Always part of the prompt sent to the model but not shown to end-users.

2. **`developer`**

   * **Who it is:** Your application or engineering team.
   * **What it does:** Provides detailed instructions, function or tool definitions (e.g. JSON-schema for an API), or higher-level orchestration hints that guide the assistant’s behavior. Think of it as the “meta-prompt” layer where you define how tools get called.
   * **Visibility:** Sent to the model but not surfaced directly to end-users.

3. **`user`**

   * **Who it is:** The human interacting with the assistant.
   * **What it does:** Contains the actual questions, commands, or conversational turns from the end-user.
   * **Visibility:** Always shown to the model as the primary input; may be logged or displayed in user interfaces.

4. **`assistant`**

   * **Who it is:** The model’s generated responses.
   * **What it does:** Carries the assistant’s output, which may be subdivided into “analysis,” “commentary,” and “final” channels (for reasoning, tool invocation, and user-facing answer).
   * **Visibility:**

     * **Analysis/commentary**: meant for internal debugging or tool orchestration and not shown to end-users.
     * **Final**: the actual reply delivered to the user.

5. **`tool`**

   * **Who it is:** A function or external service invoked by the assistant.
   * **What it does:** Returns the result of a function call (e.g., weather API, database lookup, Python execution) back into the conversation flow.
   * **Visibility:**

     * Typically hidden from end-users in raw form, but its effects (e.g., computed data) inform the assistant’s next “final” message.

---

**Summary of the flow:**

1. **`system`** sets the stage.
2. **`developer`** wires up tools and detailed instructions.
3. **`user`** asks or commands.
4. **`assistant`** reasons (`analysis`), invokes tools (`commentary`), and then speaks (`final`).
5. **`tool`** runs and returns its output for the assistant to incorporate.

This clear role separation ensures robust, transparent chains-of-thought, safe tool orchestration, and clean end-user interactions.
