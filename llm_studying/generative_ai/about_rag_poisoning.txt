**RAG poisoning** refers to **adversarial manipulation of a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) system** by injecting misleading, biased, or malicious data into its retrieval corpus. This attack can cause the model to generate incorrect, harmful, or manipulated responses.  

### **How RAG Poisoning Works:**
1. **Data Injection** – The attacker modifies the external knowledge base used by the RAG system. This can happen by:  
   - Adding fake or biased documents to the retrieval database.  
   - Editing online sources (e.g., Wikipedia, web pages) that the system scrapes for information.  
   - Poisoning vector databases by adding embeddings of misleading content.  

2. **Manipulated Retrieval** – When the system queries the database, it retrieves **poisoned** documents that rank higher than genuine sources, skewing the final response.  

3. **Generated Misinformation** – Since the LLM trusts the retrieved content, it incorporates poisoned data into its response, making the misinformation seem authoritative.  

---

### **Types of RAG Poisoning Attacks:**
✅ **Misinformation Injection:** Malicious actors insert false facts into the knowledge base, misleading users.  
✅ **Bias Amplification:** Poisoned data is designed to promote a certain ideology, brand, or political view.  
✅ **Prompt Injection via Retrieval:** The attacker inserts adversarial prompts into the retrieval database, making the model execute unintended instructions.  
✅ **Denial-of-Service (DoS) via Spam Data:** Attackers flood the retrieval corpus with irrelevant data, drowning out useful information.  

---

### **Defending Against RAG Poisoning:**
🔹 **Data Curation & Validation:** Manually verify and clean retrieved documents before using them.  
🔹 **Retrieval Filtering & Ranking:** Use trust scores, source credibility checks, and anomaly detection.  
🔹 **Adversarial Testing:** Simulate poisoning attacks to see how the system behaves under manipulation.  
🔹 **Hybrid RAG Architectures:** Combine retrieval-based knowledge with internal model reasoning to cross-check facts.
