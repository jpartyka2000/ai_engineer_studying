Yes, "Flux Kontext Pro" and "FLUX.1 Kontext [pro]" are the same model — just different ways people refer to it. The full official name is **FLUX.1 Kontext [pro]** from Black Forest Labs.

As for the differences between **Pro** and **Max**:

Pro is great for fast, step-by-step editing, keeping people and styles consistent. Max delivers sharper details and better text rendering for higher-quality generations.

**FLUX.1 Kontext [pro]:**
- Faster and cheaper
- $40 per 1,000 images
- Great for iterative editing and character consistency
- Good enough for most production work

**FLUX.1 Kontext [max]:**
- Uses more compute for improved performance compared to Pro and Dev
- $80 per 1,000 images
- Better text/typography rendering and finer detail
- Not just an impressive image editing model — it's also one of the best text-to-image models, rivaling Google's Imagen 4 in the Artificial Analysis Image Arena
- The quality improvement justifies the premium for applications where images receive close scrutiny. For quick iterations or volume work, Pro usually suffices.

There's also a **FLUX.1 Kontext [dev]** version that's open-weights (12B parameters), currently in private beta, with plans to make it open weights soon.

For your album cover use case, **Pro** is likely the sweet spot — it handles character consistency well, and you'd only need Max if you need extremely polished final output or precise text on the covers.
