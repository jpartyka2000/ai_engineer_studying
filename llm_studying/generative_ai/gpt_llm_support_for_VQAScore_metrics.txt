This is a bit of a mixed picture. OpenAI initially dropped logprobs support for GPT-5 models, which caused frustration in the developer community. However, GPT-5.1 and GPT-5.2 do appear to support logprobs — users report they can get them when structured outputs are NOT enabled, though there are bugs when combining logprobs with JSON schema mode.

So for your VQAScore use case (just asking a yes/no question and extracting the probability), GPT-5.1/5.2 should work since you don't need structured outputs. But GPT-4o remains the safest bet since it has mature, well-tested logprobs support and is what the t2v_metrics authors validated against.

Practically speaking, for your album artwork scoring:

- **GPT-4o** — proven to work, cheapest, logprobs fully supported, already validated as SOTA for VQAScore
- **GPT-5.1/5.2** — should work for simple yes/no logprobs, likely even better visual understanding, but more expensive and logprobs support is still a bit buggy
- **GPT-4o is probably the sweet spot** — it's cheaper, faster, and already demonstrated as the best VQAScore backbone. The marginal improvement from GPT-5.x on a binary yes/no visual question is unlikely to justify the cost difference
