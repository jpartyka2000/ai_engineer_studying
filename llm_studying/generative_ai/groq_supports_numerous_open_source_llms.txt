Yes, absolutely. Groq hosts open-source large language models running on its LPU (Language Processing Unit), their custom-designed chip built specifically for AI inference.

**Open-source models currently available on Groq:**

The Groq platform currently supports a broad range of open-source language models—including LLaMA 3, DeepSeek, Qwen3, and Mistral—optimized for real-time use.

They also recently added OpenAI's two open models on GroqCloud: gpt-oss-120B and gpt-oss-20B with full 128K context.

And for larger models, Groq's LPU deployed Meta's Llama 4 Maverick, a 400B parameter state-of-the-art openly-available MoE LLM, on the same day as its release.

**Why Groq is fast:**

Groq created and built the LPU from the ground up to meet the unique needs of AI. LPUs run Large Language Models and other leading models at substantially faster speeds and, on an architectural level, up to 10x more efficiently from an energy perspective compared to GPUs.

Groq supports up to 1,200 tokens/sec for lightweight models, ideal for live AI applications.

**Pricing:**

They have a free tier to get started. GroqCloud offers public APIs with usage-based pricing, and their paid tiers are competitively priced due to their hardware efficiency.

You can sign up at groq.com and get an API key to start using these models immediately. The API is OpenAI-compatible, so integration is straightforward if you've used similar services before.
