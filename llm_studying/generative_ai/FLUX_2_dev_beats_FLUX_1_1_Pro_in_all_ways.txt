Pricing
FLUX1.1 [pro] uses flat per-image pricing at about $0.04 per image VentureBeat. It's a "pro" model, so the megapixel/step formula doesn't apply — you pay a fixed rate regardless of resolution (up to its max).
FLUX.2 [dev] uses Together's megapixel-based pricing. On fal.ai it's listed at $0.012 per megapixel fal.ai, and Together.ai uses a similar structure. For a standard 1024×1024 image (~1 MP), that works out to roughly $0.012 per image — about 3× cheaper than FLUX1.1 [pro]. If you exceed the default step count, cost scales proportionally; fewer steps don't reduce cost below the default rate. Together AI
So for budget-conscious work, FLUX.2 [dev] is significantly cheaper per image.
Image Quality
These are different generations of models. FLUX1.1 [pro] is a first-gen model (12B parameter architecture) that was the flagship when it launched in late 2024 — it delivered six times faster generation than FLUX.1 [pro] while improving quality, prompt adherence, and diversity. VentureBeat It produces strong photorealistic results, good hands, and solid prompt following for a FLUX.1 family model.
FLUX.2 [dev] is a second-gen model built on a 32-billion parameter architecture with a rewritten VAE Binary Verse AI. Even though [dev] is the open-weight/budget variant of FLUX.2 (not the top-tier [pro]), it still represents a generational leap. Benchmarks show FLUX.2 [dev] achieves an ELO score of roughly 1029, putting it within striking distance of expensive proprietary models at a fraction of the cost. Binary Verse AI The improvements are most visible in lighting behavior, skin texture, fabric folds, material rendering (metal, glass), and depth of field. FLUX.2 also supports native 4MP generation FAL, while FLUX1.1 [pro] tops out around 1MP.
In practice, FLUX.2 [dev] will generally produce more detailed, more photorealistic images than FLUX1.1 [pro], especially for complex scenes.
Text Rendering
This is where the generational gap is largest. FLUX1.1 [pro] was decent at text for its era but still prone to misspellings and character swaps on longer strings. FLUX.2 represents a major improvement in embedded text, rendering legible, consistent text on posters, title cards, packaging, and UI comps with markedly fewer misspellings. Story321 The FLUX.2 family uses a Mistral-3 vision-language model internally to handle typography, which is a fundamentally different approach than FLUX.1's. FLUX.2 achieves approximately 60% accuracy on first attempt for complex typography Binary Verse AI, which sounds modest but is a massive improvement over prior generations.
Within the FLUX.2 family, [flex] is actually the text rendering specialist (it exposes step/guidance controls optimized for typography), but even [dev] substantially outperforms FLUX1.1 [pro] on text.
