## Coherence vs Fluency in LLM Evaluation

### **Fluency** - "Does it sound natural?"
Measures if the text **sounds like proper language** - grammar, spelling, natural flow.

```python
# HIGH fluency (sounds natural):
"The weather today is beautiful, with clear skies and warm sunshine."

# LOW fluency (grammatically broken):
"Weather today beautiful is, clear with skies sunshine warm and."
```

### **Coherence** - "Does it make sense?"
Measures if the text is **logically consistent** and ideas connect properly.

```python
# HIGH coherence (logical flow):
"I was hungry, so I went to the kitchen. I made a sandwich and ate it."

# LOW coherence (disconnected ideas):
"I was hungry, so I went to the kitchen. Elephants can swim. Tuesday is nice."
```

## Key Distinction:

```python
# HIGH fluency, LOW coherence:
"The CEO announced record profits yesterday. Bananas are rich in potassium. 
 Our quarterly targets were exceeded by 20%."
# Perfect grammar but ideas don't connect

# LOW fluency, HIGH coherence:
"CEO announce profit big yesterday. Company do very good this quarter.
 All employee getting bonus."
# Broken grammar but the logic is clear

# HIGH fluency, HIGH coherence (ideal):
"The CEO announced record profits yesterday. Due to exceeding quarterly 
 targets by 20%, all employees will receive bonuses."
```

## In Evaluation:

- **Fluency**: Rate the grammar/naturalness (1-5)
- **Coherence**: Rate the logical flow/consistency (1-5)

**Memory trick**: 
- **Fluency** = **Form** (how it's said)
- **Coherence** = **Content** (what it means)
