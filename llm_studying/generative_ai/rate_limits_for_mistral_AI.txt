Based on my search, Mistral AI uses **requests per second (RPS)** rather than requests per minute as their rate limit metric.

**Free tier limits:**
For all models that are compatible with the Free-tier, the following limits apply: 1 request per second

The free tier typically provides generous limits, such as approximately 1 request per second (RPS), 500,000 tokens per minute, and up to 1 billion tokens per month.

So for the free tier, that works out to roughly **60 requests per minute** (1 RPS × 60 seconds).

**Paid tier limits:**
The platform imposes rate limits of 5 requests per second, 2 million tokens per minute, and 10,000 million tokens per month, with possibilities for adjustments upon request.

That's approximately **300 requests per minute** on paid tiers.

**Key points:**
- Rate limits are set at the workspace level. Limits are defined by usage tier, where each tier is associated with a different set of rate limits.
- The free API tier is intended for evaluation and prototyping purposes. For actual projects and production workloads, we recommend upgrading to a higher tier.
- You can check your exact limits at https://admin.mistral.ai/plateforme/limits

So while Mistral offers generous **token limits** (1 billion tokens/month on free tier), the **request rate** is actually quite restrictive at 1 RPS on the free tier—more restrictive than Groq in terms of requests per minute, but more generous on total monthly token volume.
