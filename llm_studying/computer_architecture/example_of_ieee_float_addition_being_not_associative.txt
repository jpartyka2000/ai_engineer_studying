Here’s what’s happening under the hood in IEEE 754 double precision:

1. **Unbiased exponents & mantissas**  
   - **−1 × 10²⁰** encodes as  
     - exponent = 66  
     - mantissa = 0x5af1d78b58c40  
   - **3.14** encodes as  
     - exponent = 1  
     - mantissa = 0x91eb851eb851f  

2. **Aligning for addition**  
   - To add two floats, you must align their binary points—this means shifting the smaller’s mantissa right by the difference in exponents (66−1 = 65 bits).  
   - Shifting 3.14’s 52-bit mantissa right by 65 positions yields **0** (the entire mantissa falls off the right end).

3. **Result of the add**  
   - After alignment, you’re effectively doing  
     \[
       \underbrace{-1\times2^{66}\times(1.\dots)}_{\text{−1e20}} \;+\;
       \underbrace{0\times2^{66}}_{\text{from 3.14}}
       \;=\;-1\times2^{66}\times(1.\dots)\;=\;-1e20.
     \]

Because 3.14 is so much smaller than 10²⁰ (exponent difference of 65), its contribution underflows to zero in the floating‐point add, leaving you with −1e20.
