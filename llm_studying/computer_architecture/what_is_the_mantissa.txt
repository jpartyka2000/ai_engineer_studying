The **mantissa** (more properly called the **significand** in IEEE-754 parlance) is the part of a floating‐point number that encodes its significant digits.  In a normalized IEEE-754 double‐precision value you have:

```
(–1)^sign  ×  (1.fraction_bits)  ×  2^(exponent – bias)
```

- The **sign** bit is 0 or 1.  
- The **exponent** (11 bits) stores a biased power of two.  
- The **fraction bits** (52 bits) are what we call the mantissa (excluding the implicit leading “1”).  

So if the raw 52-bit fraction field is e.g. `0x91eb851eb851f` (as in our 3.14 example), that means the actual significand is  

```
1.100100111010111011100101...₂
```

i.e. the leading “1” (not stored) plus the binary point plus those 52 fraction bits. Together they determine the precision of the number’s “digits.”
