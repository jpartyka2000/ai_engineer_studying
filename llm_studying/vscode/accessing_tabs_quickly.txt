## Quick Tab Navigation in VSCode:

### **Keyboard Shortcuts (Fastest):**

1. **Cycle Through Tabs:**
   - **Next tab**: `Ctrl+Tab` (Windows/Linux) or `Cmd+Option+→` (Mac)
   - **Previous tab**: `Ctrl+Shift+Tab` (Windows/Linux) or `Cmd+Option+←` (Mac)

2. **Quick Tab Switcher:**
   - `Ctrl+Tab` and **hold Ctrl** - shows a list of all open tabs
   - Keep pressing `Tab` to cycle through
   - Release to switch

3. **Direct Tab Access:**
   - `Ctrl+1` through `Ctrl+9` - jump to tab by position
   - `Alt+1` through `Alt+9` on Mac

### **Mouse Methods:**

1. **Scroll on Tab Bar:**
   - **Hover over the tab bar** and use your **mouse wheel** to scroll left/right through tabs

2. **Tab Overflow Menu:**
   - Click the **`...`** button at the right end of the tab bar
   - Shows a dropdown list of all open tabs

### **Command Palette:**
- `Ctrl+P` (or `Cmd+P` on Mac)
- Start typing the file name to jump directly to it

### **Pro Tip:**
If you have too many tabs open, consider:
- `Ctrl+K W` - Close all tabs in the current group
- `Ctrl+K Ctrl+W` - Close all tabs
- Enable **"workbench.editor.limit.enabled"** in settings to auto-close old tabs

**Most efficient**: Use `Ctrl+Tab` to cycle or `Ctrl+P` to search by name!
