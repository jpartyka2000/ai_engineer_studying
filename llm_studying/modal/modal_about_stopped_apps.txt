Yes, you can delete stopped apps in Modal. From the dashboard you're showing, you have a couple of options:

**Via the CLI:**
```bash
modal app stop <app-id>
```
You can also list and filter them:
```bash
modal app list
```

**Via the dashboard:** Click into one of those stopped apps — there should be a delete or remove option on the app detail page (often behind a menu or at the bottom).

That said, stopped apps in Modal are mostly just historical records and don't consume resources or cost anything. They'll also eventually age off the list on their own. So unless they're cluttering your view, you don't necessarily need to clean them up.
