Yes, it can fail, but it's not risky — if the rebuild fails, nothing bad happens. You just get an error and the old cached image remains untouched. Common reasons it might fail:

- **A dependency can't be installed** — e.g., a version conflict or a package was yanked from PyPI. This would have also failed on the original build, so it's not specific to force-build.
- **Network issues** — pip can't reach PyPI, or a download times out.
- **Your local code has syntax/import errors** — though this wouldn't actually break the *build*, just the subsequent *run*.

The important thing is that force-build is **not destructive**. If it fails, your previous working image is still there. You can just fix whatever went wrong and try again, or drop the `--force-build` flag to go back to using the cached image.
