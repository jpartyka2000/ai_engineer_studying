Not really. The main costs are **time** and **compute**, not money.

**Time-wise**, looking at your image definition, a force-build will re-run all the `uv_pip_install` steps (reinstalling all those packages) and re-copy your three local directories. That's probably a few minutes depending on how long the dependency installs take.

**Money-wise**, Modal only charges for function execution time, not for image builds. So the rebuild itself is free — you just have to wait for it.

The real annoyance is that `--force-build` rebuilds **everything** from scratch, including reinstalling all your pip dependencies even if you only changed one Python file. There's no partial rebuild. That's why switching to `modal.Mount` for your frequently-changing source code can be a better workflow during active development — you keep the slow dependency installs cached in the image and only upload fresh source files at runtime.
