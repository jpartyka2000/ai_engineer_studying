The `WITH ROLLUP` clause in SQL is an extension of the `GROUP BY` clause that allows for hierarchical aggregation. It is useful for generating subtotals and grand totals for grouped data in a single query.

---

### **How It Works**
1. **Purpose**:
   - `WITH ROLLUP` calculates subtotals at various levels of grouping and a grand total for the entire dataset.
   - It extends the results of a `GROUP BY` query by including additional rows for these aggregated values.

2. **Order of Calculation**:
   - The subtotals and totals are computed based on the hierarchy of columns specified in the `GROUP BY` clause.
   - Subtotals are added for each column grouping from left to right, and the grand total is at the bottom.

3. **Null Values**:
   - The rows representing subtotals and grand totals will have `NULL` in the columns where grouping doesn't apply.

---

### **Example**
#### Dataset: `sales`
| region   | product | sales_amount |
|----------|---------|--------------|
| North    | A       | 100          |
| North    | B       | 200          |
| South    | A       | 150          |
| South    | B       | 300          |

#### Query:
```sql
SELECT 
    region,
    product,
    SUM(sales_amount) AS total_sales
FROM 
    sales
GROUP BY 
    region, product WITH ROLLUP;
```

#### Result:
| region   | product | total_sales |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| North    | A       | 100         |
| North    | B       | 200         |
| North    | NULL    | 300         |  -- Subtotal for North
| South    | A       | 150         |
| South    | B       | 300         |
| South    | NULL    | 450         |  -- Subtotal for South
| NULL     | NULL    | 750         |  -- Grand Total

---

### **Explanation**:
1. **Rows with Subtotals**:
   - For each `region`, a row with `NULL` in the `product` column shows the subtotal for that region.
2. **Row with Grand Total**:
   - A row with `NULL` in both `region` and `product` shows the grand total for all sales.

---

### **When to Use `WITH ROLLUP`**
- When you need subtotals and a grand total for hierarchical groupings.
- Common in financial or sales reports where aggregated data at different levels is required.

---

### **Limitations**:
1. **Output Format**:
   - Subtotals and grand totals appear as additional rows, which might require post-processing for user-friendly presentation.
2. **Unsupported in Some Dialects**:
   - Not all SQL dialects support `WITH ROLLUP`. In some systems, similar functionality can be achieved using `GROUPING SETS`.

---

### Advanced Example: Adding Filters with `HAVING`
You can use the `HAVING` clause to filter out subtotal or total rows if needed:
```sql
SELECT 
    region,
    product,
    SUM(sales_amount) AS total_sales
FROM 
    sales
GROUP BY 
    region, product WITH ROLLUP
HAVING 
    region IS NOT NULL OR product IS NOT NULL;  -- Exclude grand total
```

---

`WITH ROLLUP` is a powerful tool for generating hierarchical aggregates and simplifying complex reporting tasks directly in SQL.
