In a **full outer join**, it doesn't matter which DataFrame you specify as the left or right. A full outer join will include all records from both DataFrames, combining rows where the join condition is met, and including rows from either DataFrame where no match is found, filling in with `null` values.

### Key Points:
- **Full Outer Join**: This join type retains all rows from both DataFrames, ensuring that no data is lost from either side. Rows that do not have a match in the other DataFrame will have `null` values for the missing side.
- **Left vs. Right**: Since full outer joins are symmetrical, there’s no concept of "left" or "right" dominance as there is in left or right joins. Both DataFrames contribute equally to the result.

Thus, you can swap the order of the DataFrames, and the result will be the same.
