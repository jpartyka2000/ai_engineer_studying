The `--no-sync` flag tells `uv run` to skip its automatic dependency synchronization step.

By default, `uv run` checks your `pyproject.toml` (or `requirements.txt`) and ensures all dependencies are installed and up to date before running your command. This is convenient but adds a small delay to every run.

With `--no-sync`, uv skips that check entirely and just runs your script immediately.

This is useful when:
- You know your dependencies are already installed
- You want faster iteration during development
- You're running the same script repeatedly and don't want the sync overhead each time

The tradeoff is that if your dependencies are out of date or missing, you'll get an import error instead of uv helpfully installing what's needed.
