# Clarifying `max_num_seqs`

No, it doesn't mean copies of the same batch. Let me clarify the terminology:

## What `max_num_seqs` Actually Means

It's the **maximum number of independent requests/prompts** that vLLM will process simultaneously in a single forward pass.

### Example

If `max_num_seqs=8`, and you have 20 users sending prompts to your server:

- vLLM will process **up to 8 different prompts** at once
- The remaining 12 requests wait in a queue
- As requests complete, new ones are pulled in

## Key Distinction

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Sequence** | A single independent request/prompt (each user's input) |
| **Batch** | The group of sequences processed together in one forward pass |

So `max_num_seqs=8` means your **batch can contain up to 8 different sequences**—not 8 copies of one sequence.

## Why This Matters

vLLM uses **continuous batching**—it dynamically groups multiple user requests together to maximize GPU utilization. Each sequence in the batch:

- Has its own prompt
- Has its own KV cache
- Generates its own output tokens
- May be at different stages of generation

## Memory Tradeoff

Higher `max_num_seqs` means:
- ✅ Better throughput (more parallelism)
- ❌ More GPU memory (each sequence needs its own KV cache)

If you're running a large model or have limited VRAM, you might lower this value to avoid out-of-memory errors.
