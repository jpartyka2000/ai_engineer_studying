It varies widely depending on model size and precision. Here's a rough guide:

## Quick formula

A good rule of thumb: **~2 bytes per parameter** for fp16/bf16 (the most common formats).

| Model size | fp16/bf16 | fp32 |
|------------|-----------|------|
| 1B params | ~2 GB | ~4 GB |
| 7-8B params | ~14-16 GB | ~28-32 GB |
| 13B params | ~26 GB | ~52 GB |
| 70B params | ~140 GB | ~280 GB |
| 405B params (Llama 3.1) | ~810 GB | ~1.6 TB |

## Why the variation?

**Precision formats:**
- **fp32** (32-bit float) — 4 bytes per parameter, rarely used for inference
- **fp16/bf16** (16-bit) — 2 bytes per parameter, the standard
- **int8** (8-bit quantized) — 1 byte per parameter
- **int4** (4-bit quantized) — 0.5 bytes per parameter

So that same 70B model could be:
- 140 GB at fp16
- 70 GB at int8
- 35 GB at int4 (fits on a single 48GB GPU!)

## For Qwen3-8B in this code

At ~8 billion parameters in fp16, you're looking at roughly **16 GB** of weight files. This fits comfortably on an H100 (80 GB), which is why `gpu_memory_utilization=0.75` leaves plenty of room for both the weights and the KV cache.

## Why this matters

Download times, storage costs, and GPU memory requirements all scale directly with these sizes. It's why the code enables all those fast-download options — fetching 16 GB is manageable, but 140+ GB for larger models makes every optimization count.
