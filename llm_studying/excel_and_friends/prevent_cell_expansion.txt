To prevent a cell in Excel from automatically expanding when you paste text into it, you can adjust the **cell formatting**. Here are the steps to ensure the cell does not resize, even if the text overflows:

---

### **1. Turn Off "Wrap Text"**
1. Select the cell or range of cells where you want to paste text.
2. Go to the **Home** tab in the ribbon.
3. In the **Alignment** group, make sure the **Wrap Text** button is not selected. If it is, click it to toggle it off.

   - This prevents the text from wrapping to multiple lines, which could cause the row height to expand.

---

### **2. Prevent Column Width from Changing**
1. Select the cell or range of cells.
2. Right-click and choose **Format Cells**, or press `Ctrl + 1` (Windows) / `Cmd + 1` (Mac).
3. Go to the **Alignment** tab in the Format Cells dialog.
4. Under **Text control**, ensure **Shrink to fit** and **Wrap text** are **unchecked**.
5. Click **OK**.

---

### **3. Adjust the View Behavior**
If you don’t want the text to be visible outside the cell:
- Truncate the text using the **Text Alignment** settings:
   1. Select the cell.
   2. Go to **Format Cells** → **Alignment** tab.
   3. Choose a horizontal alignment like **Center** or **Right** to control how the text displays within the cell.

---

### **4. Freeze the Column Width**
To stop the column from auto-resizing when you paste text:
1. Right-click on the column header (e.g., Column A) and select **Column Width**.
2. Set a fixed width value, e.g., `10`.
3. Click **OK**.

---

### **5. Use "Overflow" Behavior Without Expanding**
If you’re okay with the text flowing into adjacent blank cells:
1. Ensure **Wrap Text** is off.
2. Keep the adjacent cells empty to allow the text to overflow visibly.

---
