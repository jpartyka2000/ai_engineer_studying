To sort all rows together in Excel (so the entire row moves, not just one column), follow these steps:

## Correct Way to Sort in Excel

1. **Select ALL your data** - Click on any cell within your data range, then press `Ctrl + A` (Windows) or `Cmd + A` (Mac) to select the entire data region
   - Or manually select from the top-left cell to the bottom-right cell of your data

2. **Go to the Data tab** on the ribbon

3. **Click "Sort"** (or use the A→Z or Z→A buttons for quick sorting)

4. **Choose your sort column** and click OK

## What Went Wrong

If only one column sorted while others stayed in place, you likely had **only that single column selected** before sorting. Excel will only sort what's selected.

## Quick Fix - Using Sort Buttons

1. Click **any single cell** within your data (don't select the whole column)
2. Go to **Data tab**
3. Click **A→Z** (ascending) or **Z→A** (descending)

Excel should automatically detect the data range and sort all columns together.

## Pro Tip - Make Your Data a Table

Convert your data to an Excel Table (select data → `Ctrl + T`):
- Adds filter dropdown arrows to headers
- Automatically includes all columns when sorting
- Prevents the "only one column sorts" problem
