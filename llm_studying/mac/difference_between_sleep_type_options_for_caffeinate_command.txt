Sure! These flags prevent different types of sleep:

- **`-d`** — Prevents the **display** from sleeping. The screen stays on.
- **`-i`** — Prevents the system from **idle sleeping**. This is the most common one — it stops your Mac from sleeping when you're not actively using it (no mouse/keyboard input).
- **`-s`** — Prevents the system from **sleeping entirely**, including when you close the lid or the system would otherwise enter full sleep mode. This is the most aggressive option.

In practice, `-i` is what most people want (e.g., keeping a long download or process alive). `-s` is stronger and can even keep the machine awake with the lid closed (on supported hardware, typically when connected to power). `-d` is specifically about the screen rather than the system itself.
